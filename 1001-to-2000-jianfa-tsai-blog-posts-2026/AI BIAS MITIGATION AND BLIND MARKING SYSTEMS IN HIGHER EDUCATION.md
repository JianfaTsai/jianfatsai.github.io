### Detailed Analysis of AI Auditing and Blind Marking
The integration of Artificial Intelligence (AI) to audit pedagogical marking patterns represents a significant shift toward "algorithmic accountability" in higher education. When a university mandates the inclusion of student names on assignments, it introduces the risk of **implicit bias**, where educators may subconsciously (or consciously) alter their grading based on a student's perceived ethnicity, gender, or social standing (Malouff & Thorsteinsson, 2016).
**The Role of AI as an Ethical Auditor**AI systems can be trained to perform "Sentiment and Consistency Analysis" across a professor's entire marking history. By comparing a specific student's work against the average quality of work that receives a similar grade from other markers, the AI can flag **statistical outliers**. For instance, if a student’s paper is linguistically and structurally similar to "A-grade" papers but receives a "C" from a specific tutor, the system triggers a manual review (Bearman et al., 2020). This prevents the "weaponization" of grades by enemies or biased faculty.
**Blind Marking and Outsourcing: A Structural Shield**The proposal to transition to **Student IDs only** effectively removes the primary trigger for identity-based discrimination. When coupled with **outsourced markers**, the university creates a "double-blind" environment. External markers, located in different states or countries, lack the local social context or personal vendettas that might lead to bribery or blackmail-induced grading (Hanes & Serdar, 2021). This globalized marking pool ensures that the evaluation is based strictly on the rubric rather than the individual’s identity.
**Economic and Social Implications**A student's Grade Point Average (GPA) is more than just a number; it is a financial asset. In competitive job markets, a low GPA—caused by biased marking—can lead to "underemployment," effectively nullifying the massive investment made in tuition fees (Walker & Bakroun, 2019). Protecting the integrity of this GPA via AI auditing is a form of consumer protection for the student.

### Counter-Arguments
While outsourcing and blind marking address bias, they introduce two primary risks:
1. **Loss of Context:** An outsourced marker may not understand specific nuances or classroom discussions that the student was expected to reference, potentially leading to lower grades for high-quality, "on-theme" work.
2. **Pedagogical Feedback Gap:** Education is a relationship. If markers are completely detached and outsourced, the feedback becomes "transactional" rather than "instructional," which may hinder student learning and development (O'Donovan et al., 2021).
### Thought-Provoking Question
If grading becomes a purely mechanical and outsourced process to ensure fairness, does the university risk losing its unique "academic culture" and the mentorship bond between professor and student?
