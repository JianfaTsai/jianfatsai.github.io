What’s the pain score (out of ten, when ten is unbearable pain and suffering) for most people when they suffer from terminal illnesses that are partly due to unhealthy lifestyle habits such as smoking, drinking alcohol, overworking, eating unhealthy processed, fast or fried foods and frequent partying?
### ELI5
For most people facing the end stages of lifestyle-linked diseases like cancer or lung failure, pain often reaches a "severe" level (7 to 9 out of 10), though palliative care works hard to bring that number down to a manageable 3 or 4.
### Terminal Pain Profiles and Lifestyle Factors
Terminal illnesses stemming from habits like chronic smoking, excessive alcohol consumption, and poor diet—often categorized as Non-Communicable Diseases (NCDs)—frequently involve high levels of physical distress. Research indicates that approximately **80% of patients with advanced-stage cancer** and **67% of those with chronic obstructive pulmonary disease (COPD)** or cardiovascular disease experience **moderate to severe pain** (World Health Organization [WHO], 2020).
On a standard Numeric Rating Scale (NRS) of 0–10:
- **0–3 (Mild):** Generally manageable; does not interfere significantly with daily activity.
- **4–6 (Moderate):** Distressing; interferes with sleep and basic tasks.
- **7–10 (Severe):** Intense, "unbearable" pain; often requires high-dose opioid intervention.
### Pain Scores by Condition
The nature and intensity of pain vary based on the specific organ systems damaged by lifestyle choices.

### The Role of Breakthrough Pain
A significant factor in terminal pain is "breakthrough pain." Even when background pain is controlled (e.g., at a score of 3), patients may experience sudden spikes reaching **9 or 10** for short durations (PMC, 2026). This is particularly common in lifestyle-related cancers that spread to the bones, where even slight movement can trigger agonizing sensations.

### Counter-Argument: The Subjectivity of Pain
While statistics highlight high pain scores, the **"Total Pain" model** suggests that terminal suffering is not purely biological. Palliative care aims to provide a "comfortable" end-of-life experience. In many modern healthcare settings, pain that would naturally be a 10/10 is mitigated through **palliative sedation** or aggressive opioid management, meaning many patients may actually spend their final days at a managed score of **2 to 4** (Better Health Channel, 2026).
### Action Steps to Improve Quality of Life
1. **Preventative Screening:** If history includes smoking or heavy drinking, regular screenings (e.g., CT scans, LFTs) can catch issues before they reach a "terminal" stage.
2. **Advanced Care Planning:** Documenting pain management preferences early ensures that medical teams prioritize comfort if a terminal diagnosis occurs.
3. **Lifestyle Mitigation:** Reducing processed food intake and managing overwork stress lowers systemic inflammation, which is a key driver of chronic pain intensity (CDC, 2026).
