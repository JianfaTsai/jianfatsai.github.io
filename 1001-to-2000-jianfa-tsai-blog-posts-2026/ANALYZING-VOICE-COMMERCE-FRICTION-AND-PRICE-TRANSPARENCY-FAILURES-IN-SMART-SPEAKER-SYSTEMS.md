### Detailed Analysis of Voice Commerce Friction

The failure of a smart speaker to articulate specific price drops—the delta between the Manufacturer's Suggested Retail Price (MSRP) and the current deal price—represents a significant breakdown in the "conversion funnel" of voice commerce (V-commerce). In the case of the Mixi 20-inch suitcase, the omission of the savings amount removes the primary psychological trigger for impulse or planned purchasing: the perception of value (Hoyer et al., 2020).

### Economic Implications and Opportunity Cost

When an AI fails to communicate a price drop, it creates an "information asymmetry" where the system knows the value proposition but the consumer does not. Over a global scale and a long-term horizon, this "bug" results in:

- **Reduced Conversion Rates:** Users are less likely to "Buy Now" via voice if they must manually check an app to verify the discount (McLean & Wilson, 2019).
    
- **Customer Churn:** If notifications lack utility (omitting crucial data like savings), users may perceive them as "spam" rather than "service," leading to the silencing of deal notifications entirely.
    
- **Compounded Financial Loss:** Even a 1% drop in conversion due to missing price data equates to billions in lost Gross Merchandise Volume (GMV) for a global entity like Amazon over several decades.

### Counter-Arguments

While the lack of price transparency is a functional flaw, some argue that voice AI is designed to be brief to avoid "information overload." A long-winded explanation of price history might cause users to stop listening entirely. Additionally, some regions have strict "was/is" pricing laws (like the Australian Consumer Law regulated by the ACCC), and if the AI is unsure of the historical price validity, it may default to silence to avoid legal liability for misleading discounts.
