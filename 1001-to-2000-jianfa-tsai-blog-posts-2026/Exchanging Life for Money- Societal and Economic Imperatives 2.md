Why do we exchange our lives for money?

### SuperGrok AI Analysis
Humans exchange life for money primarily due to capitalist structures requiring labor to meet survival needs and consumerism demands (Marx, 1867/1976; DOI: none available for original). Sociological analyses highlight how wage labor commodifies time, prioritizing financial security over intrinsic life value in modern economies (Thompson, 1967; https://doi.org/10.1093/past/38.1.56). Psychological research shows this exchange persists via habituation to extrinsic rewards despite well-being trade-offs (Kasser & Ryan, 1993; https://doi.org/10.1037/0022-3514.65.2.410).
