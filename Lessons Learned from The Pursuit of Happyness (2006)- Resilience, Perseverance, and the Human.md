### Supportive Reasoning
The film powerfully illustrates that perseverance yields results, as Gardner’s unpaid internship and relentless effort secure his future (Gardner, 2006). It underscores protecting dreams, evident in the basketball scene where the father urges his son not to accept others’ limitations (Muccino, 2006). ### Counter-Arguments
Critics contend the narrative romanticizes extreme hardship, implying anyone can replicate success through grit alone while downplaying luck, timing, and systemic racism or poverty traps (Gardner, 2006). Not every single parent enjoys Gardner’s opportunities; the film may inadvertently minimize mental health tolls or discourage seeking systemic aid. In real-world application, overemphasis on individualism risks burnout or victim-blaming.
