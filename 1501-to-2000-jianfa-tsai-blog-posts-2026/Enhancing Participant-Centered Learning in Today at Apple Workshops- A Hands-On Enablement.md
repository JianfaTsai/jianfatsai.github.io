### Problem Statement
Current Today at Apple workshops on Apple Intelligence often assume device readiness, resulting in passive observation rather than active participation for many attendees whose devices lack immediate enablement (Apple Support, n.d.-a). This gap reduces learning efficacy, inclusivity, and real-world transfer, particularly in diverse global contexts where participants arrive with varying iOS versions and device compatibility. The absence of a standardized prerequisite-activation protocol perpetuates inefficiencies across Apple’s educational ecosystem and limits monetization potential through replicable scholarly frameworks.

### Facts

### Findings
Implementing a 10-minute enablement segment at workshop outset yields immediate device personalization, model download confirmation, and feature exploration on participants’ own hardware. Global generalization standardizes this across all Today at Apple topics, elevating completion rates and satisfaction metrics. Thesis monetization potential materializes through licensing the “Prerequisite Enablement Pedagogy” model to retailers, universities, and ed-tech firms.

### Analysis
Supportive reasoning affirms the proposal enhances agency and retention through experiential immediacy, consistent with progressive education principles (Dewey, 1938/1997). Counter-arguments note potential time constraints in 60-minute sessions, device incompatibility excluding some participants, and download delays on slower networks. Balanced evaluation reveals net positive impact when facilitators prepare compatibility checklists, mitigating exclusion while preserving inclusivity. Edge cases include older devices ineligible for Apple Intelligence, requiring hybrid demo fallback strategies.

### Analysis Limitations
