### Problem Statement
In an era of economic volatility, rapid technological disruption, and proliferating financial scams, individuals often face pressures to pursue quick gains from past expertise while neglecting buffers against shocks, leading to heightened vulnerability to fraud, impulsive decisions, and asset erosion (Despard et al., 2020).

### Facts
Emergency savings correlate strongly with reduced financial stress and improved well-being, with households lacking such funds facing greater reliance on high-cost credit during crises (Consumer Financial Protection Bureau, 2022). Conservative approaches prioritize stability over speculation, as evidenced by lower volatility in value-oriented portfolios (Graham & Dodd, 1934/2008). Australian scam losses exceeded billions annually, prompting the 2025 Scams Prevention Framework (ACCC, 2025).

### Evidence
Peer-reviewed studies confirm that emergency funds mitigate debt-related anxiety and enhance resilience (Zhang, 2026). Financial literacy alone offers limited protection against fraud without digital security awareness (Ruiz et al., 2026). Value investing principles, emphasizing intrinsic worth over hype, have demonstrated enduring efficacy across market cycles (Bierig, 2000).
