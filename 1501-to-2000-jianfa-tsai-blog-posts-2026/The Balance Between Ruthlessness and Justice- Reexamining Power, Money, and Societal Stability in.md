### Problem Statement
Contemporary popular media, including the referenced YouTube video analysis of a film portraying billionaire advice, promotes the notion that severing empathy constitutes a prerequisite for wealth accumulation (Yunzhong Shuo, 2026). This perspective risks fostering misinformation by oversimplifying success as inherently ruthless, overlooking structural power held by states and the necessity of institutional order for societal function.

### Facts
Governments exercise monetary authority through central banks, enabling currency issuance subject to economic constraints rather than literal infinity. Justice systems and prisons exist universally to deter deviance and uphold the social contract, preventing the chaos predicted under pure self-interest (Hobbes, 1651). Empirical studies indicate elevated psychopathic traits among some executives, yet correlation does not establish causation for universal success.

### Evidence
Peer-reviewed analyses demonstrate that extreme wealth concentration correlates with societal ills including reduced trust and heightened incarceration, underscoring the stabilizing role of justice institutions (Eidelson, 2019). Historical and contemporary data affirm that states, not private actors, retain ultimate coercive and fiscal power.
