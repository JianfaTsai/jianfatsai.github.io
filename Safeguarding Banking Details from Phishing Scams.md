# Safeguarding Banking Details from Phishing Scams

**Glossary:**


Phishing is when scammers impersonate a trusted bank or business to steal your login details. 


Smishing refers to phishing attacks sent via text message. 


**Executive Summary:**


The user's guidance is a practical and effective defense against banking phishing scams that continue to rise in Australia. 


Adopting these habits can prevent significant financial harm and promote safer digital banking for everyone. 


**Fact Find:**


Phishing accounts for a large share of reported scams in Australia, with losses reaching around 97.6 million dollars in recent years. 


Australian banks and government agencies consistently state they will never ask for login details or send clickable links in unsolicited emails or texts. 


**Federal, State, or Local Laws in Australia:**


Under the Criminal Code Act 1995 unauthorised access to data or impairment of electronic communications can result in up to ten years imprisonment for serious fraud offences. 


The new Scams Prevention Framework passed in two thousand twenty five requires banks and other sectors to implement stronger protections and allows consumers to seek redress when obligations are not met. 


Victoria Police and state consumer laws also support reporting and investigation of such scams.
