# Library Newsletter Subscription Optimization: Enhancing Engagement, Revenue, and Compliance

**Billion-dollar library marketing insight to max profits and charity donations. **

Goodwill feedback:

Years ago, as a new library member, I ticked the Checkbox 1 to subscribe to Monash Public Libraries, VIC, Australia, events on the library member’s account at https://monlib.spydus.com

Checkbox 1: “Allow library to send marketing material to you. Select this option if you would like to receive the latest information about events at the library.”

It’s been many years since I was a new member. Perhaps I had previously unsubscribed from the newsletter email notifications through my email app. 

The problems with the marketing process (may affect Australia and global state libraries, public libraries, university libraries, school libraries, retail companies and government organisations) are:

Firstly, there’s no apparent way to resubscribe to the library event newsletter to be notified of new library events. 

I tried unchecking Checkbox 1, entering my password on the web page, saving changes, checking Checkbox 1, entering my password on the web page, and saving changes. It’s yet to be seen if this will trigger the library systems to send me future library event newsletters.

Secondly, the user’s account Checkbox 1 remains checked, even though the user has unsubscribed from the event newsletter via their email account. 

This cuts off the line of communication between the library's sales and marketing teams and potential customers, which in turn cuts off profits.

This is an important systems flaw, as it causes some users to later think they remain subscribed to event newsletters (a revenue stream for libraries, businesses, corporates, and government due to outdoor spending by the masses) when they haven’t. There’s also the knowledge barrier on what customers can do to resubscribe to library event newsletters. 

Business opportunity to sell a solution or system upgrade where the user who intends to unsubscribe from the newsletter via clicking a link in their email, where, after the website that communicates the confirmation of the newsletter unsubscription has popped up, the website sends a notification to the library systems to uncheck the Checkbox 1 in the relevant user account. 

Billion-dollar insight 2: Consider championing a research project to consider  implementing a process where, after customers have clicked on the “unsubscribe” link at the footer of the email newsletter, the website pops up to notify the user that they have unsubscribed successfully, and there are 

1. A coloured and conspicuously sized “resubscribe” button on the unsubscription website.
2. Pivotal point: the user gets sent an email to confirm their unsubscription, and there’s a coloured, conspicuously sized “resubscribe” button on the “confirmation of unsubscription” email.

This allows customers with little tech knowledge to resubscribe to the company's newsletters by simply searching for the brand name in their email app to find the “confirmation of unsubscription” email, then clicking the “resubscribe” button to resubscribe to the event or marketing newsletter subscription.

This solution can be applied not only to the library’s OPAC staff interface, but also to global retail companies and government political campaign outreach processes. 

This system flaw and business solution can be monetised as a cross-disciplinary thesis in computer science, software engineering, UX, marketing, political science, and library management. 

Please provide feedback to management and relevant stakeholders in Australia and global state libraries, public libraries, university libraries, school libraries, retail companies and government organisations.

**Glossary:** 

Spydus: Library management system by Civica used for member accounts and communications. 

CRM: Customer Relationship Management systems for tracking subscriptions and interactions. 

ILS: Integrated Library System handling catalog member data and notifications. 

**Background Information:** 

Monash Public Libraries in Victoria Australia utilize the Spydus platform for member self-service including event newsletter opt-ins via a checkbox on user accounts. 

Patrons often unsubscribe directly through email clients without the change reflecting in the library database. 

This creates a disconnect observed across many public institutions adopting digital marketing tools. 

**Relevant Federal, State or Local Laws in Australia:** 

The Spam Act 2003 mandates functional unsubscribe facilities in commercial electronic messages with requests honored within five business days without requiring extra personal information or account logins. 

Australian Privacy Principle 7 under the Privacy Act 1988 requires easy opt-out for direct marketing and consent where reasonable expectations are not met. 

Victorian public sector organizations must also comply with the Privacy and Data Protection Act 2014 emphasizing transparent handling of personal information for marketing purposes. 

Libraries as government entities benefit from certain exemptions but must still ensure consent and data accuracy. 

**Supportive Reasoning:** 

Bidirectional synchronization via APIs or webhooks between email service providers and ILS platforms like Spydus would eliminate the identified mismatch and restore accurate consent records. 

Prominent resubscribe options on confirmation pages and emails reduce friction for users with limited technical knowledge thereby recovering accidental unsubscribes and boosting long-term engagement. 

Library event marketing drives community participation attendance fees sponsorships and donations creating measurable ROI often exceeding $2-4 per dollar invested. 

**Counter-Arguments:** 

Overly conspicuous resubscribe buttons on unsubscribe pages risk appearing manipulative potentially violating the spirit of easy opt-out under the Spam Act and eroding user trust. 

Public libraries prioritize equitable access and education over profit maximization so aggressive recovery tactics may conflict with core missions. 

Integration costs for legacy systems could outweigh benefits for smaller organizations without dedicated IT resources. 

**Analysis:** 

The proposal demonstrates strong cross-domain potential in UX design marketing and library science while addressing a real-world pain point validated by industry best practices. 

Evidence from UX research supports preference centers and simple recovery flows over all-or-nothing unsubscribes. 

For Monash and similar institutions implementing the fix could serve as a model for broader Australian and global adoption. 

**Risks:** 

Non-compliance with unsubscribe rules may trigger ACMA fines up to hundreds of thousands per day for repeated breaches. 

Perceived pushiness in resubscribe prompts could increase spam complaints or negative feedback harming institutional reputation. 

Data synchronization errors might lead to unintended marketing sends violating privacy laws. 

**Improvements:** 

Implement a full preference management center allowing patrons to select event types frequencies and formats rather than binary checkboxes. 

Test the resubscribe button with A/B variations ensuring it remains secondary to clear confirmation messaging. 

Integrate real-time webhooks between the email platform and Spydus for instant account updates.
