**Glossary:**  

Operational sanitisation: The removal of physical, digital, and human traces of activities that cannot be acknowledged.  

Plausible deniability: The ability of an entity to deny responsibility for an action while maintaining operational secrecy.  

Black operations: Covert activities conducted without official acknowledgment or oversight trails.  

Rendition: The extrajudicial transfer of individuals to jurisdictions or facilities outside standard legal processes.  



**Background Information:**  

The concept emerges from intelligence and special operations communities where activities must remain hidden to avoid diplomatic fallout, legal challenges, or public backlash.  

Historical examples include post-9/11 programs involving evidence destruction and site sanitisation to maintain operational secrecy.

Under the Intelligence Services Act 2001 (Cth), the Australian Secret Intelligence Service (ASIS) may conduct activities as directed by the Minister, including those requiring deniability, provided they align with authorised functions.
