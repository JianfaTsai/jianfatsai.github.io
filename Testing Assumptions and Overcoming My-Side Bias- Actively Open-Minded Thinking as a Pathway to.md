### Abbreviations and Glossary
AOT: Actively Open-Minded Thinking – a deliberate cognitive approach that seeks disconfirming evidence and revises beliefs based on new data (Baron, 2023).  
My-Side Bias: A form of confirmation bias where reasoning favors one's own position over alternatives (Petro, 2026).  
Confirmation Bias: The tendency to search for, interpret, and recall information that confirms preexisting beliefs (Nickerson, 1998).  
Falsification: The process of attempting to disprove hypotheses rather than confirm them, rooted in scientific methodology (Popper, 2002).

### Problem Statement
Despite access to high-quality strategies, humans routinely render suboptimal decisions because cognitive biases distort evidence evaluation and assumption testing (Petro, 2026). Individuals favor supportive evidence, dismiss counterarguments, and exhibit overconfidence prior to rigorous scrutiny, which undermines learning and outcomes (Baron, 2023). This problem persists across personal, professional, and societal levels, raising questions about effective debiasing methods.

### Facts
Humans exhibit my-side bias by preferentially processing belief-congruent information (Kaanders et al., 2022). Actively open-minded thinking counters this through counterargument generation, probability assignment, and error tracking (Petro, 2026). Empirical studies confirm that such practices slow premature conclusions and accelerate learning (Baron, 2023). Bias affects even experts in high-intelligence cohorts.

### Evidence
Peer-reviewed experiments demonstrate that participants actively sample evidence supporting prior beliefs, reinforcing confirmation bias over time (Kaanders et al., 2022). Baron's (2023) framework shows AOT practitioners outperform others in decision accuracy by falsifying hypotheses. Real-world forecasting tournaments validate probability tracking as a superior method (Tetlock & Gardner, 2015). Historiographical review reveals consistent patterns from 17th-century philosophical warnings to 21st-century neuroimaging studies, with minimal temporal bias in core findings.

### Findings
Actively generating counterarguments, assigning probabilities, and tracking errors reliably reduce my-side bias and improve decision quality (Baron, 2023; Petro, 2026). Individuals and organizations adopting these practices demonstrate faster learning and fewer strategic failures. However, edge cases such as time-pressured environments or high emotional stakes limit efficacy (Kaanders et al., 2022). Nuances include cultural variations in bias expression and the need for institutional support.
