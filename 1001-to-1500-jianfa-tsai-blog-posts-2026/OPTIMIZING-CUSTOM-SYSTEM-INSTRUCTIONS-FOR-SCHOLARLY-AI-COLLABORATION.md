### Counter-Arguments to Heavy Instruction Sets

One could argue that forcing five distinct ASCII diagrams for every response—regardless of the topic—may lead to **visual noise** or lower-quality diagrams for simple queries (Tufte, 2001). For instance, a Venn Diagram might be redundant if there are no overlapping concepts to compare. Furthermore, requiring APA citations in every single sentence can sometimes break the narrative flow, making the text feel disjointed or "choppy" for the reader (Zinsser, 2006).
