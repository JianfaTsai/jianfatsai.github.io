### User's Input
**Case Study: Streamlining Student Communications at Innovate University**

**Background**  
Innovate University delivers fully online courses via its student courseware platform. Lecture webinars are automatically recorded and made available to all enrolled students. However, the initial course template and welcome email did not mention this.

**Challenge**  
Every term, students across multiple courses sent repeated emails to lecturers asking whether webinars were recorded and where to find them. This created high volumes of repetitive queries for teaching staff and support teams.

**Proposed Solution**  
Update the standard initial communication template on the courseware website to clearly state:  
- Lecture webinars are recorded and available for review.  

Include the same information in the first welcome email sent to students.

**Expected Benefits**  
- Reduces repetitive student inquiries.  
- Saves staff time and improves productivity.  
- Low-cost change with high return: audits of past digital communications showed this single tweak could cut unnecessary emails by over 70% per course.  
- Long-term gains: institutions and organisations that implement such simple proactive updates report measurable efficiency improvements and cost savings for stakeholders.

**Outcome and Recommendation**  
By making recordings availability explicit from day one, Innovate University can eliminate a common pain point, enhance student experience, and free up educator time for higher-value teaching activities.  

**Key Learning**  
Clear, upfront information in the first touchpoint prevents confusion and scales efficiently across large student cohorts. Small template updates deliver outsized productivity and financial benefits in educational settings.  

This fictional case illustrates best-practice digital communication design for universities and similar organisations.

### Action Steps
The following ten action steps provide a practical, scalable implementation plan for the proposed solution, informed by higher education communication best practices (Foral et al., 2010; TEQSA, 2022).  
1. Conduct a comprehensive audit of current course templates and welcome emails across all programs to identify gaps in information about recorded lectures.  
2. Revise the standard courseware template to include explicit statements on webinar recording availability and access instructions.

10. Establish an annual review cycle to incorporate student input and adapt communications to evolving online learning needs.
