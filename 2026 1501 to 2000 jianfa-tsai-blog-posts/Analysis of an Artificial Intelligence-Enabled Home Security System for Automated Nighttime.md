### Detailed Analysis
The AI system leverages sensors on metal shutters and window locks to detect engagement status post-10 pm, cross-referenced with occupancy data to trigger internal loud alarms only when residents are present, thereby minimizing neighbor disturbance while enforcing security protocols (Kozioł & Pławiak, 2024). Edge cases include guest scenarios where overrides prevent disruption, power failures requiring battery backups, and cultural variations in sleep schedules across Melbourne households. Nuances arise in multi-occupant homes, where AI must differentiate intentional non-securing from oversight, drawing cross-domain insights from behavioral psychology and IoT engineering (Cho et al., 2022). Implications extend to reduced burglary rates but include ethical considerations around constant monitoring. Best practices recommend hybrid notification-alarm sequences for optimal user experience, with lessons from existing smart home deployments emphasizing regular calibration (Rahim et al., 2023). Practical scalability supports individual homeowners through off-the-shelf IoT kits or organizational adoption in apartment complexes via centralized management.
