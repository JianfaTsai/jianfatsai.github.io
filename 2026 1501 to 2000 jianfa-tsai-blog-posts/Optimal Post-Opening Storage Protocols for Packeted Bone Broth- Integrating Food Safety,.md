### Problem Statement
Opened bone broth packets risk rapid microbial proliferation if mishandled, potentially leading to foodborne illness, nutrient degradation, or unnecessary waste; the user’s protocol of partial immediate use with glass-based refrigeration or freezing addresses this but requires contextual validation against Australian regulations (FSANZ, 2025).

### Facts
Bone broth qualifies as a potentially hazardous ready-to-eat food due to its protein content. FSANZ mandates refrigeration at 5°C or below for opened perishable items. Glass containers prevent chemical leaching unlike some plastics. Freezing halts bacterial growth indefinitely when properly packaged. The user’s half-and-half approach minimizes exposure time at room temperature (Food Standards Australia New Zealand, 2025).

### Evidence
Peer-reviewed guidance confirms that unpackaged potentially hazardous foods should not exceed five days at 5°C or colder post-opening (Koutsoumanis et al., 2020). Australian standards align with international benchmarks, emphasizing temperature control to inhibit pathogens such as Listeria (Ghosh, 2014). Empirical studies on stock storage validate glass as superior for maintaining sensory and nutritional quality without migration risks (Food Standards Australia New Zealand, 2025).

### Action Steps
1. Upon opening the packet, immediately divide contents equally using a clean measuring tool.
2. Heat and incorporate half into the intended recipe to minimize bacterial exposure time.
3. Transfer the remaining half into a clean, food-grade glass container with tight-fitting lid.
4. Label the container with today’s date and “use by” estimate (tomorrow for fridge).
5. Place the refrigerated portion in the coldest part of the fridge at or below 5°C.
6. For freezing, leave 2 cm headspace in the glass container to allow expansion.
7. Thaw frozen portions overnight in the fridge rather than at room temperature.
8. Consume refrigerated leftovers within 24 hours and inspect for off odors or separation before use.
9. Sanitize all utensils and surfaces post-handling to prevent cross-contamination.
10. Consult FSANZ resources annually for any updated storage guidelines.
