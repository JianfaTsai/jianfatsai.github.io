Empirical data indicate that approximately 80% of millionaires are self-made through business ownership or entrepreneurship rather than salaried positions alone (Stanley & Danko, 2000). Chua Lam's framework holds empirical support but overstates absolutes, as disciplined employee-investors can achieve millionaire status via compounding (Kukk et al., 2023).

Devil's advocate: Chua Lam's Hong Kong-centric anecdote may carry cultural bias toward entrepreneurial risk-taking, overlooking Australia's social safety nets that buffer employee stability.
