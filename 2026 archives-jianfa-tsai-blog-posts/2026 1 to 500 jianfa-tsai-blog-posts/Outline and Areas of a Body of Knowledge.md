**Glossary:**  

**BoK** — Body of Knowledge: the complete set of concepts, terms, and activities that make up a professional domain.  
**Knowledge Area** — A major category grouping related tasks and competencies within the BoK.  
**Competency** — Measurable knowledge, skills, and abilities required for proficiency.  
**Performance Domain** — Outcome-focused area in modern guides like PMBOK 7th/8th editions.  


**Background Information:**  

A body of knowledge is formally defined as the complete set of concepts, terms, and activities that make up a professional domain as defined by the relevant learned society or professional association.

**Supportive Reasoning:**  

Professional associations standardize BoKs to guide practice, certification, and continuous improvement, ensuring consistency across practitioners worldwide.  


**Counter-Arguments:**  

Knowledge is dynamic and includes tacit elements that are hard to fully codify, leading some critics to argue BoKs risk becoming outdated or overly rigid.  


**Analysis:**  

A typical BoK follows a hierarchical outline with foundational definitions, theoretical frameworks, methodologies, practical applications, standards, ethics, and interdisciplinary links.  


Professional examples include PMBOK with its 10 traditional knowledge areas maintained alongside 8 performance domains in the 7th edition (Stakeholders, Team, Development Approach & Life Cycle, Planning, Project Work, Delivery, Measurement, Uncertainty) plus 12 principles.  


BABOK structures around 6 knowledge areas (Business Analysis Planning and Monitoring, Elicitation and Collaboration, Requirements Life Cycle Management, Strategy Analysis, Requirements Analysis and Design Definition, Solution Evaluation).  


Broader academic outlines cover types of knowledge (declarative, procedural) and branches such as humanities, social sciences, natural sciences, formal sciences, and applied fields.
