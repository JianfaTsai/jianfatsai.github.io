**Federal, State, or Local Laws in Australia**  
Federal laws govern the sale of digital content in Australia, primarily through the Australian Consumer Law (ACL), which mandates that products meet acceptable quality standards, match descriptions, and provide guarantees for refunds or replacements if faulty (Australian Competition and Consumer Commission, n.d.; LawPath, 2025). The Goods and Services Tax (GST) at 10% applies to digital products sold to Australian consumers once annual turnover exceeds AUD 75,000, requiring registration with the Australian Taxation Office (ATO) and remittance of collected tax (Australian Taxation Office, 2025; Tax Window, 2025). In Victoria, state regulations align with federal standards, with no additional unique mandates for digital sales beyond general business compliance under the Competition and Consumer Act 2010 (Cth). Privacy policies and terms of service are required under federal privacy legislation to protect consumer data (LawPath, 2025). Non-compliance risks penalties, underscoring the need for accurate disclosures in content marketing.
