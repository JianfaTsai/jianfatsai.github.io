For example, a quick voice recording about a business strategy, a photo of a whiteboard sketch from a conference, and an attached PDF article on psychology might stay isolated in separate folders—yet in one canvas they remain juxtapososed, allowing AI to surface unexpected links like “how cognitive biases from the PDF apply to your strategy note.” Nuances matter: visual or spatial thinkers thrive on infinite canvases for drawing connections organically, while linear thinkers might initially feel overwhelmed without optional AI-generated views; edge cases include massive data volumes (where efficient indexing prevents slowdowns), sensitive personal information (requiring user-controlled privacy toggles), offline scenarios (favoring on-device processing where possible), or conflicting inputs (where AI flags ambiguities for human review rather than forcing a category). Implications stretch further: globally, this could save humanity vast collective hours previously spent on manual tagging and filing, freeing cognitive resources for higher-order creation, while fostering emergent insights that rigid systems suppress—consider how a live-streamed webinar clip auto-links to your three-year-old handwritten note on a related topic, sparking a novel idea neither alone would generate.
