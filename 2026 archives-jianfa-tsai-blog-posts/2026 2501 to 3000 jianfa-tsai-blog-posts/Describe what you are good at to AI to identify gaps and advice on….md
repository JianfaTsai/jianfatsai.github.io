It's not 10,000 hours that makes you good at something.  
It's how you spend those hours.  
Anders Ericsson has shown that the difference between amateurs and experts isn't just practice - it's deliberate practice.  
Working on what you're bad at.  
Getting feedback.  
Pushing past what feels comfortable.  
And honestly, it kind of sucks.  
It's frustrating. It's mentally draining. It's not fun most of the time.  
But that's the point.  
If your practice feels easy, you're probably just reinforcing what you already know.  
Real improvement feels uncomfortable.  
When you're "practicing," are you actually improving... or just repeating?
