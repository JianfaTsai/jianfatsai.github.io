**Detailed Guidance on Paraphrasing in APA 7:**    
Paraphrasing in APA 7 requires you to reword the source material thoroughly (changing both vocabulary and sentence structure) while preserving the original meaning, and you must still provide an in-text citation to credit the source. No quotation marks are placed around paraphrased content.

**Incorrect Placement (Common Mistakes to Avoid):**

**Edge Cases and Related Considerations:**    
- If your paraphrase is very close to the original wording, it may be considered too close to a quotation (sometimes called "patchwriting") and could require either more substantial rewording or conversion to a direct quote with quotation marks and a page number.    
- For the previously referenced City of Melbourne Libraries (CoML) Loans Policy, when paraphrasing specific sections (e.g., loan periods on page 4 or overdue fines on page 7), it is still best practice to include the page number in the citation for precision, even though APA 7 does not strictly require it for paraphrases: (City of Melbourne Libraries, 2024, p. 4).    
- When paraphrasing ideas that span multiple pages or sections, you may use a page range or omit the page number if the location is not critical.    
- Always ensure the paraphrased content accurately reflects the source to avoid misrepresenting the author's intent, particularly in policy documents where precise rules matter for compliance.    
- International nuances: While APA 7 is widely adopted in Australian universities (including those in Melbourne, Victoria), some institutions may have supplementary guides that encourage page numbers for all citations from paginated documents like PDFs to enhance traceability.
