📊 **Power Laws of Business (2001) by Richard Koch** – where to read/buy ebook or cheapest physical copy.  
**Power Laws of Business (PLB)** is a 2001 revised paperback edition (ISBN 9781857882547, ~320 pages, Nicholas Brealey Publishing) by Richard Koch exploring universal patterns from science (physics, biology, mathematics) that drive success in business, markets, and organizations, building on his earlier work like *The 80/20 Principle*.  
**Pareto Principle (80/20 rule)** is the observation that roughly 80% of effects come from 20% of causes, often applied in business to focus efforts on high-impact areas.

**Wise humans**: “Focus on the vital few rather than the trivial many – the essence of power laws is leverage through selectivity.” – echoing Koch’s own principles and thinkers like Vilfredo Pareto or modern strategists who apply scientific patterns to decision-making. Warren Buffett and Charlie Munger have similarly stressed mental models from multiple disciplines for business success.  
**Counter-arguments**: Critics note that power laws (e.g., heavy-tailed distributions in markets) can over-simplify complex human systems, ignoring randomness, black-swan events, or ethical concerns of extreme inequality they sometimes describe. Some find Koch’s applications inspirational but not rigorously predictive, better as heuristics than formulas. In volatile economies (like post-2001 or today’s tech/AI shifts), rigid application risks missing emergent opportunities outside “universal” patterns.
