Gaps persist in cross-cultural applications, though preliminary evidence suggests universality tempered by contextual norms.

### Detailed Analysis
The principle that people withhold assistance from unlikable individuals operates through mechanisms of reciprocity and affective bias, where positive emotions lower barriers to prosocial action (Sanchez, 2022). Nuances arise in virtual versus face-to-face interactions, with digital cues amplifying or diminishing perceived warmth. Implications extend to leadership, where likable figures secure greater follower buy-in, while counterexamples from authoritarian contexts reveal short-term compliance without genuine help.
