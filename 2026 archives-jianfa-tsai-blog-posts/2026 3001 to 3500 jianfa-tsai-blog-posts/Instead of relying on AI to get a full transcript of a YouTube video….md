Instead of relying on AI to get a full transcript of a YouTube video, which can be helpful in some use cases but actually creates shallow comprehension and detracts from long-term memory of the content, it might be more useful to use Wispr Flow to take notes of the video.  
  
Specifically, when you watch a YouTube video and you listen to or see a useful idea or single sentence, the first sentence, then you pause the video and voice dictate in your own words a paraphrase of that sentence into your note-taking app. Check and edit the dictation for errors. Pause and ponder on the dictated text, add additional thoughts and commentary to note. Go back to the YouTube video and play the video by pressing the space bar key. Repeat the process for a second sentence. Use Chrome extension Scribber citation tool to copy the YouTube video in APA7 referencing format to paste into notetaking app below your note.
