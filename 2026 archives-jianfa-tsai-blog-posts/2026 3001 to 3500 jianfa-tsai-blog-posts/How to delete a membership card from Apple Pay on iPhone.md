How to delete a membership card from Apple Pay on iPhone?  
  
AI Analysis: Membership cards, also known as loyalty cards or rewards cards, are non-payment passes stored in the Apple Wallet app (not to be confused with actual credit or debit cards used for Apple Pay (Apple Pay) transactions). Removing them is a straightforward process that helps declutter your digital wallet without affecting payment functionality.  
🗑️** Delete Membership Card from Apple Wallet! **🗑️  
**ELI5:** Just like throwing away an old paper loyalty card, you open your phone’s Wallet app, tap the card you don’t want anymore, and hit a button to remove it forever.  
**Step-by-step instructions to remove a membership or loyalty card (pass) from Apple Wallet on iPhone:**  
1. Open the **Wallet** app on your iPhone. (Important: Launch it directly from the home screen or by searching for “Wallet” — do **not** open it by double-clicking the side button, as that shows the quick Apple Pay (Apple Pay) payment sheet where removal options may be limited.)  
2. Tap the membership card (or any pass like a rewards card, ticket, or boarding pass) that you want to delete.  
3. Tap the **More** button (the circle with three dots ⋯) in the top-right corner of the screen.  
4. Tap **Remove Pass** (for non-payment cards like membership/loyalty cards).  
5. Confirm the removal when prompted.  
Once removed, the pass is deleted from your iPhone and may also be removed from any paired Apple Watch. You can usually re-add the membership card later by scanning a new code from the store or app that issued it.  
**Related considerations and nuances:**  
* **Payment cards vs. passes:** If you’re trying to remove a credit or debit card (used for actual Apple Pay (Apple Pay) payments), the steps are slightly different: After tapping the card and the More button, select **Card Details**, scroll down, then tap **Remove Card**.  
* **Expired or old passes:** Some users report difficulty removing old loyalty cards or tickets if they only access Wallet via the side button. Always use the full Wallet app.  
* **Edge cases:** If the **Remove Pass** option doesn’t appear, try restarting your iPhone, updating to the latest iOS version, or signing out/in of iCloud (though this affects payment cards more). In rare cases, contact the issuer of the membership card for assistance.  
* **Bulk or alternative removal:** There isn’t a direct bulk delete for all passes, but you can hide expired ones in some views or remove them one by one. For lost devices, you can remove items remotely via iCloud.com under your Apple Account > Devices > Wallet & Apple Pay section.  
* **iPad or other devices:** On iPad, go to Settings > Wallet & Apple Pay to manage some cards, but passes are primarily handled in the Wallet app on iPhone.  
**Wise humans:** “Digital minimalism teaches us that regularly auditing our virtual possessions, much like clearing physical clutter, frees mental space and improves daily efficiency. Treating membership cards with the same care as physical ones prevents unnecessary data accumulation and potential security exposure from outdated entries.”  
**Counter-arguments:** Some argue keeping old passes is harmless since they don’t consume significant storage or pose major privacy risks if unused, and re-adding them later might be inconvenient if the original issuer no longer provides easy access.
