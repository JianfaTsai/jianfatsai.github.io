Wispr Flow (the AI-powered voice-to-text dictation app by Wispr that turns spoken words into polished, formatted text in any app via a simple Fn key hold or hands-free shortcut) currently forces you to Command+Tab or mouse-click between Chrome (for the full YouTube video) and your note-taking app because the Fn activation only inserts text into the currently focused text field. Your proposed dual-MacOS-device solution—Mac Mini running Chrome + YouTube on its display while the MacBook Neo handles the note-taking app + Wispr Flow dictation via its built-in keyboard—**does eliminate that friction entirely** and is a faster workflow once set up, but it is not the simplest or cheapest path for most people.    
  
Here’s a complete breakdown with multiple angles, edge cases, and related considerations:    
  
**How the two-device setup works in practice**    
- Place the Mac Mini (video machine) and MacBook Neo (notes machine) side-by-side or stacked so both displays are visible at once.    
- Open YouTube in Chrome on the Mac Mini (full-screen or large window) and let it play continuously.    
- On the MacBook Neo, open your note-taking app, place the cursor in the text field, and use its Fn key (default Wispr Flow hotkey) or Fn+Space (hands-free mode) to dictate while watching the video on the other screen.    
- No app switching, no focus loss on either device—the video keeps playing independently, audio comes from the Mini’s speakers or external setup, and your voice goes straight into notes via the MacBook’s microphone.    
- Linking is automatic if both Macs are signed into the same Apple ID, on the same Wi-Fi, and Bluetooth-enabled (Continuity features handle background sync). You do **not** need Universal Control unless you want to share one keyboard/mouse; using two separate keyboards (MacBook’s built-in + any external on the Mini) keeps everything truly independent and matches your “two keyboards” description exactly.    
- Notes sync instantly via iCloud if your note-taking app supports it, so everything ends up in one place.    
  
**Speed gains and real-world performance**    
This setup removes every Command+Tab or mouse movement—dictation becomes a zero-click background task while your eyes stay on the video. In high-volume note-taking sessions (e.g., lectures, research videos, long-form content) it can save dozens of micro-interruptions per hour. Edge case: if you occasionally need to pause/scrub the video, you simply reach to the Mini’s keyboard or use its trackpad—no context switch on the notes machine.    
  
**Downsides, costs, and edge cases**    
- **Hardware & space**: You need two displays visible simultaneously (Mac Mini usually requires an external monitor; MacBook Neo uses its own screen). Desk real estate doubles, plus power draw, cable management, and potential heat/noise. If you don’t already own both devices, this is expensive.    
- **Audio & microphone**: Video sound from Mini speakers could bleed into the MacBook’s mic; use headphones on the Mini or position devices farther apart. Wispr Flow’s AI is excellent at noise rejection but not perfect in loud environments.    
- **Portability & battery**: MacBook Neo stays plugged in or drains faster; Mac Mini is stationary.    
- **Setup friction**: Initial alignment of windows, ensuring Wi-Fi/Bluetooth stability, and testing mic directionality. Rare Continuity hiccups (e.g., devices go to sleep) require waking both.    
- **Overkill risk**: If you only do this occasionally, the extra hardware feels like a sledgehammer for a thumbtack.    
  
**Stronger single-Mac alternatives (often faster to implement and zero extra cost)**    
1. **YouTube Picture-in-Picture (PiP) mode** — Right-click the YouTube video twice (or use the PiP button in controls) to pop a floating, resizable video window that stays on top of **every** app, including your full-screen note-taking app. Keep cursor in notes, press Fn on Wispr Flow, and dictate while watching the floating video—no switching ever. Resizable, movable, and works in Chrome/Safari.    
2. **Split View or Stage Manager** — Snap Chrome and notes side-by-side (green full-screen button → Tile Window to Left/Right). Video and notes visible simultaneously; dictate without leaving the notes side.    
3. **Multiple Desktops/Spaces** — Video in Space 1, notes in Space 2. Control+Left/Right arrow or trackpad swipe switches instantly (far fewer steps than Command+Tab).    
4. **Wispr Flow built-in Scratchpad or Quick Notes** — Dictate into a floating Wispr window first, then paste/copy to your main app later—reduces focus switches.    
5. **External monitor on single Mac** — Video on external display, notes on MacBook screen—same visual separation as dual Macs but one computer, one keyboard.    
  
These single-Mac options usually solve 90 % of the friction in under 30 seconds of setup and require no new purchases.
