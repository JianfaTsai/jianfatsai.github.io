### Analysis of AI-Driven Nutritional Risk Assessment
Integrating AI into the assessment of long-term health risks for specific consumables requires a structured, multi-dimensional approach. Current AI models can process vast datasets—including longitudinal cohort studies, toxicological reports, and metabolic pathways—to identify correlations that human researchers might overlook (Harvard T.H. Chan School of Public Health, 2023). However, the "workflow" must be optimized to ensure the AI distinguishes between transient physiological responses and chronic disease development, such as cardiovascular disease, Type 2 diabetes, or neurodegeneration (World Health Organization, 2024).

### Counter-Arguments
While AI offers speed, it is often plagued by the "Black Box" problem, where the reasoning behind a risk prediction is opaque. Furthermore, AI can hallucinate correlations in small datasets, potentially labeling a harmless food as "high risk" due to confounding variables in the training data (Stanford University, 2025). AI cannot replace clinical physical examinations or the nuanced judgment of a registered dietitian who understands a patient's full medical history.
