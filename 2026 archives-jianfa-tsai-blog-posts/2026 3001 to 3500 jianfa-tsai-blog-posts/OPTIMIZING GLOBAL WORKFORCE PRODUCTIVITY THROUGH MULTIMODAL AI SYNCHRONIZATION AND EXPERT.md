### Analysis of the Multimodal AI Learning Ecosystem
The proposed system represents a shift from passive consumption to active, high-fidelity knowledge synthesis. By leveraging Multimodal Large Language Models (MLLMs), the platform addresses the "transient information effect," where learners lose key details during live transitions between speech and visual aids (Mayer, 2021).
#### The Pivotal Discrepancy Mechanism
The core innovation lies in the automated detection of non-verbatim speech. In pedagogical settings, professors often provide "extra-textual" information—anecdotes, edge cases, or updated data—not present on the static PowerPoint. By highlighting these gaps, the AI ensures that the user’s attention is directed toward the unique value-add of the live session rather than redundant slide reading (Sweller et al., 2019).
#### Infinite Canvas & Spatial Cognition
Utilizing an infinite canvas allows for "spatial hypertext," which mirrors the way the human brain organizes complex mental models. By color-coding concepts (e.g., blue for definitions, red for contradictions, green for exam-related content), the system reduces the cognitive load required to navigate large datasets (Shipman & Marshall, 1999).

### Counter-Argument: The "Black Box" and Quality Control Risk
While the AI identifies misinformation, the "hallucination" rate of LLMs remains a concern. Relying on an AI to flag teacher errors may lead to "false positives" where the AI misunderstands pedagogical nuance or metaphorical language (Bender et al., 2021). Furthermore, the pay-per-question model may exacerbate the "digital divide," where only wealthy students/workers have access to top-tier human experts.
