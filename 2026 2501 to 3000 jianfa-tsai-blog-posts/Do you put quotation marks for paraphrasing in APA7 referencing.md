Do you put quotation marks for paraphrasing in APA7 referencing?  
  
AI Analysis: In American Psychological Association (APA) 7th edition style, the in-text citation for a direct quotation is placed after the closing quotation mark but before the ending punctuation of the sentence, ensuring the citation clearly attributes the quoted material without interrupting the quoted text itself.

**ELI5:** When you copy someone's exact words in quotes, you put the citation right after the closing quote mark but still inside the full stop at the end of the sentence – like the quote is the star and the citation is the polite credit tag that comes right after.  
  
**Detailed Guidance on Placement (APA 7):**    
For a short direct quotation (fewer than 40 words), the correct format places the in-text citation **after the closing quotation mark** and **before the sentence's final punctuation**. This rule applies whether the citation is parenthetical or narrative.    
  
**Correct Examples:**    
- Parenthetical citation: "The borrowing period for most items is 21 days" (City of Melbourne Libraries, 2024, p. 4).    
- Narrative citation: City of Melbourne Libraries (2024) state that "the borrowing period for most items is 21 days" (p. 4).    
  
**Incorrect Placement (Common Mistakes to Avoid):**    
- "The borrowing period for most items is 21 days." (City of Melbourne Libraries, 2024, p. 4) ← Citation should not appear after the full stop.    
- "The borrowing period... (City of Melbourne Libraries, 2024, p. 4)" ← Citation inside the quotation marks is not APA style.    
  
**Edge Cases and Related Considerations:**    
- For quotations of 40 words or more (block quotations), the citation appears after the final punctuation of the block quote, with no additional quotation marks. Example:    
  > The policy outlines clear consequences for overdue items...    
  (City of Melbourne Libraries, 2024, p. 7)    
- When the quoted material already ends with a question mark or exclamation point, retain that original punctuation inside the quotes and place the citation after the closing quotation mark, followed by the sentence's period.    
- If integrating the author's name narratively, the page number still follows the closing quote.    
- For the City of Melbourne Libraries (CoML) policy PDF previously referenced, always include the page number for direct quotes to allow precise location of the borrowed text.    
- International variations: While APA 7 is primarily American English, the citation placement rule remains consistent globally; however, some Australian universities may have minor house-style preferences – always check your institution's guidelines.  
  
**Wise humans:** Seasoned academic writers and APA style trainers from university writing centres emphasize that precise citation placement demonstrates respect for intellectual property and enhances readability, preventing any ambiguity about where the author's voice ends and the citer's analysis begins; they advise practising with real documents like council policies to internalise the habit early in one's academic or professional journey.  
  
**Counter-arguments:** Some informal writing guides or older citation styles (such as certain pre-APA 6 variants or discipline-specific adaptations) occasionally placed citations differently or tolerated minor deviations for aesthetic flow, arguing that strict adherence can sometimes disrupt sentence rhythm; however, APA 7 maintains the post-quotation placement as the standard to ensure consistency and traceability across scholarly work.
