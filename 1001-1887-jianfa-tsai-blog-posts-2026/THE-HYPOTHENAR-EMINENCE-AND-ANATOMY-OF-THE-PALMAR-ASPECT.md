### Detailed Anatomical Analysis

Based on the provided file "image.png", the red "X" is located on the **hypothenar eminence**. This is the muscular protrusion located on the ulnar (pinky finger) side of the palmar surface of the hand.

#### Muscular Composition

The hypothenar eminence is composed of a group of four intrinsic muscles that primarily control the movements of the fifth digit (little finger):

- **Abductor digiti minimi:** Responsible for pulling the little finger away from the other fingers.
    
- **Flexor digiti minimi brevis:** Functions to flex the little finger at the metacarpophalangeal joint.
    
- **Opponens digiti minimi:** Allows the little finger to touch the thumb (opposition).
    
- **Palmaris brevis:** A superficial muscle that wrinkles the skin on the ulnar side of the palm, improving grip.

### Counter-Arguments and Nuance

While the term **hypothenar eminence** refers specifically to the muscular bulk, a clinician or palmist might refer to the general area differently depending on context. For instance, in chiromancy (palm reading), this region is often called the **Mount of Moon** (Luna). Anatomically, one might also refer to this as the **ulnar border of the palm**, though this describes the edge rather than the fleshy mass itself.
