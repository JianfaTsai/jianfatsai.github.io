### Facts
Precision in thinking involves deliberate metacognitive practices that align with Socratic questioning techniques, as evidenced in critical thinking scholarship (Paul & Elder, 2012). Vague language often stems from imprecise word choice and can be mitigated through structured rewriting exercises supported by meta-analytic reviews of writing instruction (Graham, 2007). Cognitive endurance, or the capacity to sustain focused attention on complex material, correlates positively with habits of attentive reading of longer texts and negatively with skimming behaviors (van de Ven et al., 2023). Without targeted strategies, these challenges hinder personal and professional growth, perpetuating cycles of intellectual disengagement.

### Abbreviations and Glossary
Socratic Method: A form of cooperative argumentative dialogue based on asking and answering questions to stimulate critical thinking and illuminate ideas.  
Metacognition: Awareness and control of one's own thinking processes.  
Cognitive Patience: The ability to sustain focused attention and delay gratification during reading or problem-solving (van de Ven et al., 2023).  
Perseverative Thinking: Repetitive focus on negative thoughts that may impede clear comprehension (Stade et al., 2023).

Digital distractions may undermine these strategies, rendering them less effective without environmental controls (van de Ven et al., 2023).
