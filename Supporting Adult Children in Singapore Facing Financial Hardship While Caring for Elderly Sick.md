**Glossary:**  

Home Caregiving Grant (HCG): Monthly cash payout to offset costs of caring for seniors with moderate to severe disabilities at home.  

Activities of Daily Living (ADLs): Basic self-care tasks such as feeding dressing toileting and mobility.  

ComCare: Ministry of Social and Family Development financial assistance programme for low-income individuals and families.  

Caregiver Burnout: Physical emotional and mental exhaustion from prolonged caregiving responsibilities.  

Respite Care: Temporary relief services allowing caregivers short breaks.

**Counter-Arguments:**  

Means-testing for schemes such as the Home Caregiving Grant with a per capita income cap of S$4,800 may exclude borderline hardship cases or delay support during application processing periods of six to eight weeks.  

The Maintenance of Parents Act although considerate of a child’s financial capacity can still generate family conflict or tribunal proceedings adding emotional and legal stress (Ministry of Social and Family Development n.d.).  

Full-time caregiving without mandatory paid leave often leads to career sacrifices and deepened financial strain as illustrated in the CNA Insider case where depression persisted despite family efforts (CNA Insider 2022).  

Limited subsidised respite availability particularly outside peak hours may hinder consistent self-care for working caregivers.

**Free Action Steps:**  

1. Assess eligibility for the Home Caregiving Grant and apply immediately via the Agency for Integrated Care e-FASS portal using Singpass.

2. Contact the nearest Social Service Office to enquire about ComCare assistance tailored to caregiving-related income loss.

3. Join free caregiver support groups through the Agency for Integrated Care or Institute of Mental Health for peer advice and emotional relief.

4. Schedule personal health check-ups and allocate daily time for short walks or mindfulness to practise immediate self-care.

5. Discuss care-sharing responsibilities with siblings or extended family to distribute duties equitably.

**Fee-Based Action Steps:**  

1. Engage a certified care management consultant (approximately S$150 to S$300 per session) to develop a personalised long-term financial and care plan.

2. Enrol in advanced caregiver training courses beyond the subsidised S$400 grant if specialised skills are required (fees vary by provider).

3. Hire short-term private respite care or a part-time domestic helper leveraging the S$60 levy concession to secure dedicated breaks (market rates apply).

4. Consult a financial planner specialising in eldercare and retirement (initial consultation fees typically S$200 to S$500) to optimise CPF and insurance strategies.
