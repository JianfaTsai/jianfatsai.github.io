### Problem Statement

### Action Steps
1. Begin by reviewing your last month's bank statements to establish a baseline of current spending patterns (Tan, 2024).
2. Create a simple pen-and-paper daily expense log, recording every transaction accurately starting tomorrow.
3. Categorize all expenses into broad groups and identify the single largest category for a 50% reduction target over the next month.
4. Implement one small weekly change, such as waiting seven days before any non-essential purchase, to build gradual discipline (Barrafrem, 2024).
5. Ensure monthly expenses remain below income by automating a small transfer to savings on payday.
6. Reconcile credit card statements against receipts weekly to catch discrepancies immediately.
7. Introduce family budgeting education by assigning age-appropriate personal expense responsibilities during the next household meeting.
8. Reward successful weekly adherence with a small, non-financial treat, such as a family walk, to reinforce positive habits (Boto-García et al., 2022).
9. Review progress monthly, simplifying the budget further if adherence wanes, and adjust for any Australian-specific costs like utilities.
10. Consult free resources from ASIC or MoneySmart.gov.au quarterly to refine strategies against regulatory or economic shifts.
