### Explain Like I’m 5
Imagine a big game of chess where some pieces are fancy kings who boss everyone around and others are simple pawns who just move forward. After the game finishes, you put every single piece—king or pawn—back into the exact same box. No one stays special. Then new kids play the same game all over again, but maybe with different toys or in a different room. Life works like that too: we all end up in the "box" (the ground) someday, and kids grow up to play similar life roles.

### Analogies
This proverb parallels the medieval European "Dance of Death" artworks, where skeletons lead kings and peasants in equal procession to the grave, underscoring vanitas themes (Clark, 1950). It also echoes Ecclesiastes 3:19-20 in the Bible, stating that humans and animals share the same fate of returning to dust, independent of status. In modern terms, it resembles the "level playing field" of a cemetery, akin to how economic studies show inheritance cycles often reset or replicate inequalities across generations without addressing root mortality (Piketty, 2014).
