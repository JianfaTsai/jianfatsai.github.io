### Facts

In Australia, consumer financial protection frameworks emphasize informed decision-making to prevent over-indebtedness from lifestyle spending (Australian Treasury, 2012).

### History
Consumer society emerged in Western Europe during the eighteenth century amid the Industrial Revolution, shifting cultural norms from material restraint rooted in religious values toward identity formation through goods acquisition (Roach, 2019). Historians note that pre-industrial households prioritized communal traditions and class-based display limits, whereas post-1970s deregulation of credit and advent of direct-marketing channels transformed homes into commercial spaces (Roach, 2019). In Australia, post-World War II economic expansion paralleled global trends, with rising homeownership and interior decoration marketing reinforcing consumption as a marker of success. Critical inquiry reveals temporal bias: early accounts often romanticized frugality, while contemporary historiography critiques consumerism’s environmental and psychological costs (Roach, 2019). The user’s principle echoes pre-consumerist restraint yet adapts it to modern investment vehicles.
