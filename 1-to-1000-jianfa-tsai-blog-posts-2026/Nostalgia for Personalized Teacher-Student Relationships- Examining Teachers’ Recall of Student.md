### Problem Statement
Contemporary educational environments often prioritize standardized testing and large enrollments over individualized recognition, leading to diminished teacher-student rapport compared to earlier eras when smaller, community-based schools facilitated name recall by heart (De Nobile, 2021). This erosion risks reduced student motivation and belonging, particularly in diverse Australian contexts where cultural name sensitivity matters (Victorian Department of Education, 2023).

### Evidence
Empirical data from life sciences education contexts demonstrate that students who perceive instructors know their names report higher levels of comfort, participation, and course performance (Cooper et al., 2017, https://doi.org/10.1187/cbe.16-08-0265). Australian studies confirm similar patterns, with name usage linked to stronger classroom management and reduced behavioral issues (De Nobile, 2021). Longitudinal evidence from university settings shows sustained relational effects when names are prioritized early (Hagenauer & Volet, 2014).
