### Glossary:

Responsibility: The ethical duty to utilise one's capabilities, whether physical, intellectual, or positional, to benefit society rather than solely for self-interest.  

Heroism: Acts of courage and sacrifice performed without expectation of reward, often involving personal cost to protect others.  

Scientific Hubris: Overconfidence in technological or biological advancement without adequate ethical safeguards leading to unintended harm.  

Vigilantism: Self-appointed enforcement of justice outside legal frameworks, which may conflict with authorised authorities.  

Resilience: The capacity to recover from adversity, such as grief or failure, through adaptive learning and perseverance.  

### Background Information:

The Amazing Spider-Man, released in 2012, is a United States superhero film rebooting the Spider-Man franchise starring Andrew Garfield as Peter Parker.  

Directed by Marc Webb, it follows a high school student bitten by a genetically engineered spider who gains superhuman abilities, including wall-crawling and web-shooting.  

Peter investigates his parents' mysterious disappearance, encounters Dr Curt Connors at Oscorp, and inadvertently aids in creating the Lizard antagonist.  

After his Uncle Ben's death at Peter’s earlier inaction, the protagonist adopts the Spider-Man mantle to combat crime and ultimately prevents a citywide catastrophe.  

The narrative emphasises Peter's internal growth from impulsivity to mature heroism amid romantic tensions with Gwen Stacy and conflicts with authority figures.
