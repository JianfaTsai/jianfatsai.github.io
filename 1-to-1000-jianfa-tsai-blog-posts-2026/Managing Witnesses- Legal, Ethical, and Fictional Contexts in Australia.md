**Relevant Federal, State or Local Laws in Australia:**  

Under federal law, the Crimes Act 1914 (Cth) Section 36A prohibits intimidation of witnesses in federal judicial proceedings with penalties up to 5 years imprisonment.  

In Victoria, the Summary Offences Act 1966 Section 52A makes it an offence to harass witnesses with penalties of up to 120 penalty units or 12 months imprisonment.  

The Crimes Act 1958 (Vic) Section 257 addresses intimidation or reprisals relating to criminal investigations with up to 10 years imprisonment.  

Interference with witnesses also constitutes common-law contempt of court with no statutory maximum penalty.  

Perverting the course of justice carries up to 25 years in some jurisdictions.
