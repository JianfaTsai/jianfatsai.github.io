**Glossary:**  

Reward Points: Loyalty currency redeemable for goods or services, typically valued at approximately 0.5 cents per point in major Australian programs.  
Trade Promotion Lottery: Chance-based giveaway tied to promoting retail trade under Australian regulations.  
AMOE: Alternative Method Of Entry allowing free participation to ensure legal compliance.  

  

**Background Information:**  

Australian retailers have successfully run similar high-value points promotions for over a decade, with programs like Woolworths Everyday Rewards and Coles Flybuys featuring one-million-point jackpots to combat cost-of-living pressures.  

  

**Relevant Federal, State or Local Laws in Australia:**  

All promotions must comply with the Australian Consumer Law prohibiting misleading conduct or failure to provide prizes as offered.  

In Victoria, no permit is required for trade promotion lotteries, provided there is no entry fee and the promotion promotes the business.  

Prizes valued over $1,000 require winner name publication via website or Victorian newspaper within 28 days of draw.  

National campaigns must also satisfy varying permit rules in NSW, QLD and other states depending on prize value and chance element.  

  

**Supportive Reasoning:**  

High-value points prizes create strong FOMO and directly link to increased basket size through purchase-linked entries.  

Historical data from similar campaigns shows measurable uplift in transaction volume and loyalty program adoption.  

  

**Counter-Arguments:**  

Critics may argue the low probability of winning discourages participation or that points have limited perceived value without clear dollar equivalence.  

Campaign costs including prize liability and compliance could exceed returns if entry volume is low.  

  

**Analysis:**  

The campaign integrates seamlessly with existing ERP loyalty modules by automating entry tracking via receipt scans and app data.  

Cross-domain linkage to customer relationship management systems enables personalised follow-up communications for non-winners.  

  

**Risks:**  

Potential ACL breaches from unclear terms leading to fines up to millions for corporations.  

Redemption liability if multiple winners or system errors inflate points issued.  

Data privacy concerns under Australian Privacy Principles if customer details are mishandled.  

  

**Improvements:**  

Add gamification layers such as daily instant-win multipliers to broaden appeal beyond the grand prize.  

Include explicit dollar-value messaging such as “1 Million Points = approximately $5,000 in shopping value” for transparency.
