### History
Srinivasa Ramanujan (1887–1920), born in colonial Madras, India, developed theorems independently before contacting G. H. Hardy in 1913; their collaboration during World War I produced over 30 papers until Ramanujan's return to India and early death (Kanigel, 1991). The film condenses this era, highlighting 1914–1919 Cambridge amid wartime xenophobia, with historiographical evolution shifting from Western-centric views to decolonized appreciations of non-European contributions (Alladi, 2016).
