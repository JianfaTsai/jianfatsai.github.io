### Problem Statement
Global economies face persistent skill gaps that limit productivity and innovation, while traditional textbook distribution channels dilute publisher revenues and limit learner access to high-quality, evidence-based study skills resources (Hanushek & Woessmann, 2020). Public awareness of metacognitive strategies remains low despite their proven impact on academic and professional outcomes, creating an opportunity for targeted digital education campaigns to drive direct purchases from university presses and simultaneously elevate workforce capabilities (Gust et al., 2024).

### Facts
University presses such as Harvard University Press publish titles like *Make It Stick: The Science of Successful Learning*, which synthesize decades of cognitive research into practical study techniques. Social media and YouTube platforms reach billions of users daily, enabling low-cost, scalable content distribution. International shipping of books to Australia incurs a 10% GST on low-value imports under AUD 1,000 with zero customs duty for printed books, facilitating feasible home delivery (Australian Border Force regulations, as documented in consumer guidance). Direct-to-consumer sales from university bookstores increase publisher margins compared to third-party retailers by eliminating intermediary discounts.

### Evidence
Empirical studies demonstrate that cognitive skills explain more variance in economic growth than years of schooling alone, with a one-standard-deviation increase in skills associated with approximately two percentage points higher annual GDP growth (Hanushek & Woessmann, 2020). YouTube marketing case studies reveal that educational content creators achieve significant engagement and conversion rates when demonstrating practical techniques before recommending resources (Rival IQ, 2016). Peer-reviewed analyses confirm that direct publisher sales enhance customer relationships and provide valuable market feedback unavailable through wholesale channels (Scholarly Kitchen, 2014).
