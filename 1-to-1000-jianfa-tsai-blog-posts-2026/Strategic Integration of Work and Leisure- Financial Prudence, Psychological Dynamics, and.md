### Federal, State, or Local Laws in Australia
The Australian Consumer Law (Schedule 2 of the Competition and Consumer Act 2010) provides nationwide guarantees for travel services and prohibits misleading conduct in promotions or FX disclosures (ACCC, 2021). In Victoria, Consumer Affairs Victoria enforces fair trading under state legislation, including refunds for unsatisfactory services. ASIC regulates financial advice and FX products, requiring licensed providers to act in clients’ best interests, though personal family exchanges fall outside formal oversight unless scaled commercially. No laws mandate wealth thresholds for travel; consumer protections focus on transparency rather than prescriptive budgeting.
