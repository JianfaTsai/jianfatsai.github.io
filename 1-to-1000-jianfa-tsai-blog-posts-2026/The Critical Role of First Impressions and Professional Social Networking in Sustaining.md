### Supportive Reasoning

### Counter-Arguments
Critics note that over-reliance on first impressions can perpetuate biases and overlook merit-based performance, as initial judgments often stem from superficial cues rather than sustained contributions (Maliqi, 2025). Excessive networking may appear inauthentic, potentially damaging credibility if perceived as manipulative (Al-Shatti & MacDonald, 2021). Moreover, the *Suits* dramatization simplifies complex dynamics; real-world contexts involve structural factors like organizational culture that first impressions alone cannot overcome (InfiniteWealthLabOfficial, 2026, dramatized representation).

### Discussion
Balancing the evidence reveals that while first impressions provide a critical head start, their durability depends on ongoing authentic networking rather than performative actions (Swider et al., 2022). Cross-domain insights from psychology and management highlight the need for self-awareness to mitigate confirmation bias in both forming and receiving impressions.

### Risks
Poor first impressions risk immediate marginalization, while superficial networking can erode trust and invite scrutiny for inauthenticity (Maliqi, 2025). In extreme cases, repeated negative signals may accelerate disengagement or termination.

### Immediate Consequences
Within weeks, weak initial networking can result in exclusion from key projects or informal information channels, directly impacting performance evaluations (Arnau-Sabatés et al., 2020).

### Long-Term Consequences
Over years, unaddressed poor impressions compound into stalled promotions or voluntary turnover, limiting career mobility and professional reputation (Swider et al., 2022).

### Research Gaps
Limited longitudinal studies examine the interaction of first impressions with digital networking platforms in hybrid Australian workplaces. Further inquiry is needed on cultural variations in impression formation.

### Improvements
Professionals should prioritize genuine curiosity in conversations, seek mentorship early, and maintain consistent follow-through to reinforce positive impressions (Al-Shatti & MacDonald, 2021).
