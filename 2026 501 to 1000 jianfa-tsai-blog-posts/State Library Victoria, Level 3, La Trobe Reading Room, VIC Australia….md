# State Library Victoria, Level 3, La Trobe Reading Room, VIC Australia.  
#   
Writers speak for those kept in silence. A book is not merely one that reads, but one that reads us. Blessed are those privileged to read what they like — a reflection of our true, articulate selves alive and aware. Education is a lifelong journey. To read well, one must be an inventor. No one has the final word.  
  
Books are the threads woven into the fabric of our cultures and civilisation.  
  
One reads to ask questions.  
  
Books are the children of the mind.  
  
Words inspire millions.  
  
Reading all good books is like conversing with the greatest minds of past centuries.  
  
To understand a nation, read its words.  
  
One cannot contribute to humanity without knowledge; only a person with a free soul, unshackled by fear, can make a real difference.  
  
No two people read the same book.  
  
Today, the true university is a collection of books.  
  
Vast, independent reading and self-education matter most.  
  
Books are the plane, train, road, destination, and journey.  
  
Let us preserve old books and study them with care.  
  
Read to truly live.  
  
Books are the world’s treasured wealth and the rightful inheritance of generations and nations.
