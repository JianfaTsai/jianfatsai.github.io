### Facts
Singapore’s Budget 2026, delivered by Prime Minister Lawrence Wong on February 12, 2026, introduced targeted cost-of-living relief measures, including a one-time Cost-of-Living Special Payment ranging from S$200 to S$400 for approximately 2.4 million eligible adult Singaporeans, alongside S$500 Community Development Council (CDC) vouchers for 1.4 million households scheduled for January 2027 and enhanced U-Save utilities rebates (Ministry of Finance [MOF], 2026).  
The ongoing 2026 Middle East conflict, involving escalations with Iran and disruptions to the Strait of Hormuz, has driven global energy and commodity price increases, resulting in higher inflation forecasts for Singapore and elevated costs for electricity, transport, and daily necessities (Gan Kim Yong, 2026; Straits Times, 2026a).  
On April 7, 2026, the government announced an additional support package valued at nearly S$1 billion on top of Budget 2026 commitments; this included increasing the Cost-of-Living Special Payment to S$400–S$600 (disbursed in September 2026), advancing CDC vouchers to June 2026, and providing S$200 cash relief to eligible platform workers, private-hire car drivers, and taxi drivers starting April 2026 (Siow, 2026; Reuters, 2026).  
Prime Minister Wong explicitly acknowledged these measures in his April 19, 2026, NTU follow-up video, emphasizing that “no one will have to go through this alone” while urging mental preparedness for sustained high energy prices (Wong, 2026).  
Eligibility for the enhanced payments generally requires Singaporean citizenship, age 21 and above, assessable income up to S$100,000, and ownership of no more than one property (MOF, 2026).
