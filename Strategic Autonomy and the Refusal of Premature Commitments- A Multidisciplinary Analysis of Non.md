### Supportive Reasoning
Strategic non-commitment preserves autonomy and reduces exploitation risk by allowing information asymmetry in one’s favor (Curhan et al., 2022). Silence creates curiosity, prompting others to invest cognitive effort and reveal more, thereby improving outcomes (mdscae, 2026). Psychologically, this aligns with trust models emphasizing reputation before closeness (Hancock et al., 2023). In uncertain environments, such restraint functions as self-protection, mirroring evolutionary caution toward strangers.

### Counter-Arguments
Excessive caution may foster isolation or signal distrust, limiting collaborative opportunities and network growth (Launay et al., 2015). Over-reliance on non-shackling could impede genuine relationship formation, as shared vulnerability often precedes trust (Hancock et al., 2023). In fast-paced professional settings, rigid boundaries might appear uncooperative, reducing perceived likability and joint value creation.
