### Facts
Peer-reviewed data establish that cyclists in Australia face higher relative risks of injury and fatality compared to car occupants when measured per kilometer traveled (Garrard, 2010). In Melbourne, cyclist serious injuries increased by 109% between 2000 and 2008, while car occupant injuries declined (Garrard, 2010). Nationally, cyclists represent approximately 3% of road fatalities despite comprising a smaller share of total travel (Boufous et al., 2013). Multi-vehicle crashes predominate, often involving inadequate passing distances or driver inattention (Boufous et al., 2012). However, absolute annual cyclist fatalities average around 36 nationally, indicating low overall incidence (Boufous, 2016). Health benefits include reduced all-cause mortality and improved cardiovascular fitness, with studies demonstrating consistent positive associations (Oja et al., 2011).
