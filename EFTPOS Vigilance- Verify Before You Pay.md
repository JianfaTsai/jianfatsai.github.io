**Glossary:**  

EFTPOS: Electronic Funds Transfer at Point Of Sale, the Australian system for card payments including contactless taps.  


ACL: Australian Consumer Law, the national framework protecting buyers from misleading pricing and unfair practices.  



**Background Information:**  

Contactless payments surged in popularity across Australia following the 2010s, enabling faster transactions but sometimes reducing customer scrutiny of displayed amounts.  


Cashier errors, whether accidental keying mistakes or rare intentional overcharges, occur in busy retail, cafe, and restaurant environments.  



**Relevant Federal, State or Local Laws in Australia:**  

Under the Australian Consumer Law (Schedule 2 of the Competition and Consumer Act 2010), businesses must not engage in misleading or deceptive conduct regarding prices, including displayed transaction amounts.  


In Victoria, Consumer Affairs Victoria enforces these rules alongside the ACCC, requiring accurate proof of transaction for purchases over $75 and clear display of any surcharges.  



**Supportive Reasoning:**  

Checking the screen catches common errors like transposed digits or unapplied discounts before funds leave the account.  


It empowers consumers and aligns with ACCC guidance on price displays, fostering trust in everyday transactions.  



**Counter-Arguments:**  

Routine verification may slow down queues and feel unnecessary when most transactions process correctly.  


Some terminals have limited customer visibility, and habitual trust in staff reduces perceived need.  



**Analysis:**  

The benefits of pre-payment verification outweigh the minor inconvenience, especially given the low effort required and rising contactless adoption.  


Data from consumer forums and ACCC reports indicate errors remain a persistent issue despite technological advances.  



**Risks:**  

Failing to check risks immediate financial loss from overcharges that may require bank disputes or refunds.  


Longer-term, repeated unaddressed errors erode consumer confidence in local businesses.  



**Improvements:**  

Businesses could enhance staff training on accurate keying and install clearer dual-facing screens.  


Consumers should combine screen checks with requesting itemised receipts for all transactions.  



**Wise Perspectives:**  

Vigilance in small matters builds financial resilience over time.  


As the ACCC notes, informed consumers drive fairer market practices.
