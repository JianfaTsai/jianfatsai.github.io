### Counter-Arguments
Despite surface appeal, the advice rests on astrological foundations widely classified as pseudoscience due to its lack of falsifiability, mechanistic explanation, and refusal to engage disconfirming evidence (Thagard, 1978). Studies of Chinese zodiac beliefs reveal superstitious influences on corporate risk-taking without objective validity, potentially amplifying bias rather than insight (Fisman et al., 2022; Lu et al., 2020). Historiographically, such horoscopes often serve commercial intent on social media, risking misinformation by presenting untested generalizations as wisdom. Over-reliance could discourage evidence-based financial planning, as controlled motivation (external pressure from “fate”) correlates negatively with well-being compared to autonomous strategies (Di Domenico et al., 2022).
