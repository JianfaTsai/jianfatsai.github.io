### Glossary
- **Tidal bore**: A powerful, wall-like wave created when ocean tides clash with a river’s outflow, producing dramatic surges up to nine meters high.
- **Qianlong**: The fourth emperor of China’s Qing Dynasty (reigned 1735–1796), renowned for six official inspection tours through the culturally rich south.
- **Jiangnan**: The prosperous “south of the river” region below the Yangtze, including Hangzhou, celebrated for its scenery, poetry, and economic importance.
- **Qiantang River**: Major waterway in Zhejiang Province famous worldwide for its extreme tidal bores, especially during autumn.
- **Su Shi (Su Dongpo)**: Influential Song Dynasty poet and official (1037–1101) whose works often praised natural wonders and are commonly linked to descriptions of the Qiantang tide.
