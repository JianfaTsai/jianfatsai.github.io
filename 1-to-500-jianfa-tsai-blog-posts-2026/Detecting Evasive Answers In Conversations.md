**Glossary:**  


Evasion refers to the deliberate avoidance of a direct answer to conceal information or discomfort.  


Deflection is redirecting the conversation away from the original question often by counter-questioning.  


Plausible deniability involves crafting responses that allow avoidance of accountability.  



**Background Information:**  


Evasive behavior commonly arises in situations involving fear of judgment, protection of privacy, or maintenance of secrets.  


Psychological research highlights it as a response strategy in interpersonal conflicts or high-pressure interrogations.  


The video illustrates a character trained in black-ops who evades loved ones to preserve operational security.  



**Relevant Federal, State or Local Laws in Australia:**  


This topic has limited direct legal applicability in casual conversations as Australia does not criminalize general evasiveness.  


However in court testimony under oath perjury laws under the Crimes Act 1958 (Vic) or Commonwealth Criminal Code may apply if evasion constitutes misleading justice with penalties up to 10-15 years imprisonment depending on jurisdiction.  


Evasive answers in legal settings require follow-up as per precedents emphasizing intent and materiality.  



**Supportive Reasoning:**  


Multiple cues such as answering with another question or providing vague ambiguous responses consistently signal avoidance according to communication psychology sources.
