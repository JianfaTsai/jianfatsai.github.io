**Glossary:**  

RCD: Residual Current Device, a safety switch that cuts power during faults.  

Li-ion: Lithium-ion battery technology common in modern devices.  

AS/NZS 3000: Australian and New Zealand Wiring Rules standard for electrical installations.  


**Background Information:**  

Daily routines involving showers often coincide with device usage or charging needs, prompting habits that separate electricity from moisture to reduce risks.  


**Relevant Federal, State or Local Laws in Australia:**  

Under the national AS/NZS 3000:2018 Wiring Rules, bathrooms are divided into zones where power outlets require 30mA RCD protection and are prohibited or restricted near water.  

In Victoria, Energy Safe Victoria enforces compliance, with unlicensed electrical work attracting fines up to $20,000 and serious breaches up to $300,000 in some jurisdictions.  

No direct penalty exists for user-level pre-shower charging advice, but general duty of care under occupational health and safety laws applies to household electrical practices.  


**Supportive Reasoning:**  

Charging beforehand avoids handling plugs with potentially damp hands post-shower and prevents devices from entering humid bathroom air that can cause corrosion.  

It supports battery longevity by allowing controlled charging cycles outside high-humidity zones.  


**Counter-Arguments:**  

The advice may overgeneralize, as modern devices with fast charging or optimal 20-80 percent battery management do not require rigid pre-shower timing.  

It could inadvertently encourage leaving chargers in or near bathrooms, increasing long-term moisture exposure risks.  


**Analysis:**  

Balancing the input reveals strong alignment with core electrical safety principles yet highlights flexibility needs based on individual usage patterns and technology advancements.  

Overall, it promotes sensible habit formation without contradicting standards.  


**Risks:**  

Ignoring the tip might lead to wet-hand interactions with live outlets or device damage from steam ingress.  

Rare but documented electrocution incidents occur globally when plugged devices contact water.  


**Improvements:**  

Adopt smart plugs or timers for automated charging in dry areas, and integrate with apps that monitor battery health proactively.  

Pair the habit with a dedicated dry-zone charging station outside the bathroom.
