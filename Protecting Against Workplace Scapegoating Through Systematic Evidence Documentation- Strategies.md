### Action Steps
1. Establish a daily chronological incident log (date, time, location, participants, objective facts, witnesses, outcomes) using a secure, password-protected digital template or notebook.
2. For every email or instruction received, reply immediately with "As per your instructions on [date/time]..." attaching prior communications and request written confirmation.
3. Print and duplicate all key documents (emails, approvals, warnings) monthly, storing one set in a bank safe deposit box and another in a home safe with chain-of-custody notes.
4. Timestamp and categorize evidence explicitly: emails, instructions, approved changes, ignored warnings, decisions (by whom/when), and responsibilities, maintaining objectivity without emotional language.
5. Conduct weekly self-audits of documentation completeness, cross-referencing against team problems like miscommunication to identify patterns early.
6. Secure digital backups via encrypted cloud services compliant with Australian privacy laws, ensuring offsite redundancy without employer network access.
7. Consult a union representative or Fair Work Ombudsman anonymously before escalating, using evidence to support general protections claims.
8. Develop a personal "evidence retention schedule" (e.g., retain 7 years for potential disputes) and review annually for legal updates under Fair Work Act amendments.
9. Train on private investigation best practices (e.g., from recommended textbooks) for discreet observation of team dynamics without surveillance violations.
10. Debrief quarterly with a trusted mentor to evaluate documentation efficacy and adjust for edge cases like remote work.
