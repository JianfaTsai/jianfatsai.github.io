**Case Study: Name Association Bias in Charity Marketing**

**Concept 1: Psychological Priming and Negative Associations**  
In a February 2026 charity lottery brochure for a fictional "City General Hospital," a patient testimonial used the name "Max." (Same principle on using the patient's name as Rob, which sounds like Robbery.) This triggered subconscious links to "max out" or theft-related terms for some demographics, evoking fear, anxiety, and trauma. Result: reduced donation intent.

**Concept 2: Customer Demographics and Emotional Response**  
Target audiences with past crime experiences showed lower engagement. Cognitive bias amplified negative word associations, decreasing charity participation.

**Concept 3: Business Impact on Non-Profits**  
Lower donations reduced organizational revenue, limiting salary growth and leaders' personal charitable contributions.

**Key Learning**  
Test names and language for unintended connotations in marketing materials to protect donation rates. Applicable to marketing, psychology, and charity management studies.
