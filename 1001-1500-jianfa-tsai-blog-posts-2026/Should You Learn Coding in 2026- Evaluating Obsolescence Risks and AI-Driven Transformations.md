### SuperGrok AI Analysis
The Channel News Asia Deep Dive video from March 2026 featuring Singapore's Minister of State for Education Janil Puthucheary emphasizes the need for students to master foundational skills like computational thinking before relying on AI tools, directly countering passive dependence on technology in education and future work (Channel News Asia, 2026). Learning coding now builds irreplaceable problem-solving and logical reasoning abilities that enable effective collaboration with AI systems rather than replacement by them, as evidenced by studies showing that generative AI augments rather than eliminates core programming competencies (Sauvola et al., 2024). Coding knowledge will not become obsolete in five years because software development roles are evolving toward AI orchestration, system architecture, and ethical oversight where human judgment remains essential for debugging complex outputs and ensuring contextual accuracy (Denny et al., 2024). Pros of learning coding include enhanced productivity through AI tool integration, transferable computational thinking skills applicable across disciplines, and expanded career opportunities in high-demand hybrid tech roles with reported salary premiums of 13-25% for AI-augmented developers (Zviel-Girshin et al., 2024). Cons involve the requirement for continuous upskilling amid rapid technological changes and potential challenges for entry-level positions focused solely on routine coding tasks that AI can automate more efficiently (Lepp et al., 2025). The widespread claim in popular discourse that AI has rendered coding skills entirely obsolete represents misinformation, as rigorous academic analyses confirm that fundamentals enable critical evaluation and innovation with generative tools instead of fostering over-reliance (Banh, 2025). To improve this analysis and correct assumptions of full obsolescence, prioritize hands-on practice with AI-assisted coding platforms alongside traditional fundamentals to align personal skills with the evolving 2026 job market demands for hybrid expertise.
