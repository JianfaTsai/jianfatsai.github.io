### ELI5 Summary
To be really good at anything, you need to make sure you know the basic rules perfectly before trying the hard stuff, and you should check those basics every few months so your brain doesn't forget them.
### The Science of Foundations and Review
The principle of mastering foundations before advancing is rooted in **Educational Scaffolding** and **Cognitive Load Theory**. According to cognitive science, higher-level concepts are built upon "chunks" of foundational knowledge (SongguoTV, 2026). If these chunks are not securely stored in long-term memory, the brain becomes overwhelmed when trying to process complex information, leading to a "cognitive bottleneck" (Paas & van Merriënboer, 2020).
The **Forgetting Curve**, pioneered by Hermann Ebbinghaus, suggests that humans lose approximately 70% of new information within 24 hours if no attempt is made to retain it. By implementing a **Quarterly Review Cycle**, learners engage in **Spaced Repetition**, which strengthens the neural pathways associated with that data, effectively "resetting" the curve and moving information into permanent storage (Smolen et al., 2016).

### Counter-Arguments and Nuance
While mastery of foundations is critical, an over-fixation on the "perfect" foundation can lead to **Analysis Paralysis** or "The Tutorial Trap," where a learner never actually applies their knowledge to real-world problems. Sometimes, "just-in-time" learning—learning a foundational concept only when a higher-level problem requires it—can be more efficient for adult learners who need immediate practical application (SongguoTV, 2026).

### Action Steps for Improvement
1. **Personal:** Audit your current hobbies. Identify one "basic" skill you've struggled with (e.g., proper form in exercise) and dedicate next week to mastering just that.
2. **Academic:** Schedule "Review Days" in your calendar for every three months. Use these days exclusively to revisit earlier textbooks or introductory papers (APA 7).
3. **Work:** Create a "Cheat Sheet" of the core terminology and KPIs of your industry. Review it every quarter to ensure your strategic decisions are based on accurate fundamental data.
