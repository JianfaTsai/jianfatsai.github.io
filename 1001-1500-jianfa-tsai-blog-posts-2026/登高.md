**Full Poem (Original Chinese)**  
**登高**  
杜甫（唐代，大历二年，767年）  

风急天高猿啸哀，  
渚清沙白鸟飞回。  
无边落木萧萧下，  
不尽长江滚滚来。  
万里悲秋常作客，  
百年多病独登台。  
艰难苦恨繁霜鬓，  
潦倒新停浊酒杯。  

（古文岛, n.d.；百度百科, n.d.）

**English Translation of the Poem** (adapted from Xu Yuanchong’s poetic rendition)  
The wind so swift, the sky so wide—apes wail and cry;  
The isle so clear, the sand so white—birds wheel and fly.  
The boundless forest sheds its leaves shower by shower;  
The endless river rolls its waves hour after hour.  
A thousand miles from home, I’m grieved at autumn’s plight;  
Ill now and then for years, alone I’m on this height.  
Living in times so hard, at frosted hair I pine;  
Cast down by poverty, I have to give up wine.  
(Chinese Idioms, n.d.; see also chinesetolearn.com, 2012)

**背景简述** (for context): The poem was composed in autumn 767 CE (Dali 2nd year) in Kuizhou (modern Fengjie, Chongqing). At age 56, Du Fu was in exile after the An Lushan Rebellion, having lost patronage following Yan Wu’s death. Suffering chronic illness and poverty, he climbed a high terrace alone on the Double Ninth Festival, inspiring this masterpiece often called the “crown of seven-character regulated verse” (七律之冠). (百度百科, n.d.)

**分析 (in Mandarin)**  
《登高》是唐代伟大诗人杜甫的代表作之一，作于大历二年（767年）秋天，当时杜甫56岁，身处夔州，生活困苦，疾病缠身，独自登高远眺，触景生情，写下这首被誉为“七律之冠”的杰作。前四句写景：首联描绘近景，风急天高中猿声哀鸣，水清沙白处鸟儿回旋，渲染萧瑟秋意；颔联写远景，无边落叶萧萧而下，不尽长江滚滚东流，展现自然之壮阔与无常，暗喻时光流逝与个人命运。后四句抒情：颈联“万里悲秋常作客，百年多病独登台”点明主旨，诗人漂泊万里，长期作客他乡，暮年多病，孤独登台，悲秋之情油然而生；尾联“艰难苦恨繁霜鬓，潦倒新停浊酒杯”总结一生坎坷，国难家愁使白发丛生，又因病戒酒，愁绪更难排遣。全诗情景交融，对仗工整，声律和谐，体现了杜甫“沉郁顿挫”的诗歌风格。通过这首诗，我们可以学习到：1. 面对人生困境时的坚韧与自我反思；2. 自然景观如何激发深刻的情感共鸣；3. 诗歌作为表达内心世界和时代精神的强大工具；4. 对历史人物苦难的同情与对生命的珍惜。（古文岛, n.d.；百度百科, n.d.）

**English Translation of the Analysis (Second English Translation)**  
"Climbing High" is one of the representative works of the great Tang Dynasty poet Du Fu. Composed in the autumn of the second year of Dali (767 CE), when Du Fu was 56 years old, living in Kuizhou in poverty and illness, he climbed high alone, and inspired by the scenery, wrote this masterpiece known as the "crown of seven-character regulated verse." The first four lines describe the scene: the first couplet depicts a close view with fierce wind and high sky, mournful ape cries, clear waters and white sands where birds circle, rendering a desolate autumn atmosphere; the second couplet shows the distant view with boundless leaves rustling down and the endless Yangtze rolling onward, displaying nature's grandeur and transience, metaphorically suggesting the passage of time and personal fate. The latter four lines express emotions: the third couplet "Ten thousand li of sorrowful autumn, constantly a sojourner; a hundred years of many illnesses, alone climbing the terrace" reveals the theme, with the poet drifting ten thousand li, long a guest in foreign lands, in old age full of ailments, climbing alone, evoking autumnal melancholy; the final couplet "Hardships and bitter regrets have multiplied the frost on my temples; despondent, I have recently stopped [drinking from] the muddy wine cup" summarizes a lifetime of adversity, national and family woes causing graying hair, and illness forcing abstinence from wine, making sorrows harder to dispel. The entire poem blends scene and emotion perfectly, with exquisite parallelism and harmonious rhythm, embodying Du Fu's "profound and restrained" poetic style. From this poem, we can learn: 1. Resilience and self-reflection in the face of life's hardships; 2. How natural landscapes evoke profound emotional resonance; 3. Poetry as a powerful tool for expressing inner worlds and the spirit of the times; 4. Sympathy for historical figures' sufferings and cherishing life. (Translated from 古文岛, n.d.; 百度百科, n.d.)

**What Can I Learn from the Poem?**  
The poem offers timeless lessons on human resilience amid adversity—Du Fu confronts personal exile, chronic illness, and national turmoil yet transforms suffering into enduring art. It highlights the impermanence of life (via falling leaves and rolling river) while celebrating nature’s majestic power, teaching us to find beauty and catharsis in desolation. Structurally, its perfect couplets and scene-emotion fusion (情景交融) demonstrate how poetry can universalize individual pain, fostering empathy and reflection on aging, loneliness, and the passage of time. Readers today can apply these to cultivate gratitude, perseverance, and creative expression during personal challenges. (Baidu Baike, n.d.; 古文岛, n.d.)
