How do I technically know, beyond a shadow of a doubt, whether the view count and comments of my social media photos, videos, and posts are from real humans or from AI bots?

You usually cannot prove with 100% certainty whether every view or comment came from a real person or a bot.

Even platforms themselves sometimes struggle with that.

What you can do is look for behavioural patterns, because fake engagement almost always behaves differently from real attention over time.

One thing many people misunderstand is that bots are no longer just obvious spam accounts posting “Nice pic 🔥.” A lot of fake engagement today is designed to imitate normal behaviour. That is why surface-level numbers alone are becoming less reliable.

The strongest signal is consistency between actions.

Real audiences usually create connected behaviour patterns. If someone watches content, there is a higher chance they eventually revisit the profile, interact again later, view stories, share posts, or engage across multiple pieces of content over time.

Bots rarely create that type of natural continuity.

That is why some accounts have impressive-looking numbers but weak overall audience behaviour. High views with almost no profile visits, random comments without meaningful interaction, sudden engagement spikes from unrelated regions, or large follower growth without long-term engagement stability are usually warning signs.

Another thing I keep noticing is that fake engagement often lacks behavioural depth.

Real people hesitate, scroll back, rewatch, click profiles, disappear for days, then return later. Human behaviour is naturally inconsistent.

Bots tend to behave in patterns that are either too fast, too repetitive, or too disconnected from normal audience activity.

Comments are another strong clue.

Real comments usually reference something specific, react emotionally, continue conversations, or sound slightly imperfect. Bot comments often stay strangely generic because they are designed to work on almost any post.

And honestly, one of the biggest signs is delayed collapse.

Artificial engagement can inflate visibility temporarily, but weak audience behaviour usually catches up later. Reach becomes unstable, retention weakens, and future posts struggle because platforms eventually notice that the interaction patterns are not behaving like real audience interest.

That is why real engagement tends to compound while fake engagement usually fades.

Real audiences create memory.
Bots mostly create numbers.
