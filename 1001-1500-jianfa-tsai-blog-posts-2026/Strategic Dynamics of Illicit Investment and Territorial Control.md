### ELI5 (Simple Explanation)
While a safe city protects your money from thieves, it also makes it easier for the "good guy" police to see where that money came from and take it away forever.
### The Paradox of Rule of Law in Organized Crime
In fiction and reality, a "stable rule of law" is a double-edged sword for a criminal enterprise. While it provides protection against petty theft and contractual breaches, it introduces **institutional transparency** and **regulatory oversight** that can be more lethal to a crime boss than a rival gang.
### 1. The "Paper Trail" vs. The "Protection Racket"
A stable legal system requires documentation. Legitimate businesses in high-transparency zones (like Singapore or Zurich) demand rigorous Know Your Customer (KYC) protocols and Anti-Money Laundering (AML) checks.
- **The Exposure Risk:** Moving large sums of "dirty" capital into a stable economy triggers automated red flags.
- **Asset Forfeiture:** In a stable rule-of-law environment, the state has the power to freeze and seize assets without a "street fight." A boss can fight off a rival with guns, but he cannot shoot a legal injunction (Reuter, 1983).
### 2. The Cost of Enforcement
In "unstable" regions, the boss _is_ the law. He doesn't pay taxes; he pays bribes.
- **Private Ordering:** In the absence of state enforcement, criminals rely on "private ordering"—using reputation and violence to enforce deals (Gambetta, 1993).
- **Comparative Advantage:** A crime boss has a competitive advantage in a chaotic environment because he possesses the "means of coercion." In a stable city, his skill set (violence) is a liability, not an asset.
### 3. Sunk Cost and Geographic Loyalty
Criminal empires are often built on **territorial monopolies**. Leaving a "lawless" city means abandoning the infrastructure of corruption—the judges, dockworkers, and police chiefs already on the payroll. Starting fresh in a stable city requires building a legitimate reputation from scratch, which is a slow and low-margin endeavor compared to the high-margin returns of illicit trade.

### Counter-Arguments: Why He _Should_ Move
- **Generational Wealth:** Most "first-generation" crime bosses eventually try to legitimize to ensure their children don't live in violence.
- **Diversification:** Keeping all assets in a war zone is bad portfolio management. Even a 5% "legit" return in London is better than a 50% "dirty" return that might get burned down tomorrow.
