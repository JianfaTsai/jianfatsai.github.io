### Multiple Angles, Nuances, and Counter-Arguments

While the governmental push for organ donation is framed as a universal good, there are valid counter-arguments and nuances to consider regarding why families decline. Some demographics express a deep distrust of the medical establishment or harbour fears about premature declarations of death to harvest organs (Neate et al., 2015). Furthermore, religious or cultural beliefs surrounding the sanctity of maintaining an intact body after death can heavily influence a family's decision to override a registration (Neate et al., 2015). Additionally, the sheer emotional trauma and exhaustion families face when a loved one is in an intensive care unit can make processing complex medical requests overwhelming, leading to a default refusal. Acknowledging these anxieties is critical for healthcare providers to facilitate better, more empathetic end-of-life conversations.
