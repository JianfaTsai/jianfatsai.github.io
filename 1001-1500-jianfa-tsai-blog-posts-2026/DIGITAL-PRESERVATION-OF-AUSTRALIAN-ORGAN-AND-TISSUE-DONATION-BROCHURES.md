### **CRITICAL ANALYSIS OF THE CURRENT WORKFLOW**

Your existing workflow is structurally sound, leveraging **high-contrast backgrounds** and **asymmetric lighting** to maximize Optical Character Recognition (OCR) potential. However, scanning semi-glossy government brochures involves specific optical challenges such as "hot spots" and geometric distortion that can confuse AI extraction models (Raghu, 2020). By refining the environmental variables and software selection, the accuracy rate of the subsequent AI analysis can be significantly improved.

### **SYSTEMIC CHALLENGES AND COUNTER-ARGUMENTS**

- **The "Shadow" Paradox:** While side-angled lighting reduces glare, it can create "micro-shadows" in the gutter (crease) of the brochure. This causes the AI to misinterpret text near the fold.
    
- **Counter-Argument:** Using a **clear acrylic sheet** (non-reflective) to flatten the brochure is often superior to lighting adjustments alone. Physical flattening eliminates the geometric distortion that software must otherwise "guess" to fix, leading to higher categorical accuracy during extraction (PDF Lab, 2026).
    
- **Privacy Implications:** Uploading government health brochures to third-party AI platforms may inadvertently link your identity to specific medical interests in the platform’s training data.
