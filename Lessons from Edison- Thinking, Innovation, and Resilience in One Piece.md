**Supportive Reasoning:**  

Edison's constant brainstorming under Seraphim attacks demonstrates innovation thrives in crisis when paired with action.  

His empathetic concern for Stussy and opposition to York's greed highlight intellect aligned with moral good rather than isolation.  

Rebuilding the combined Vegapunk body and floating Punk Records offshore proves resilience through adaptation and data continuity.  

**Counter-Arguments:**  

Edison's distraction by ideas risks overlooking immediate physical dangers, as seen in near-misses during battles.  

The satellite system itself illustrates fragmentation of genius, potentially leading to internal betrayal (York) if ethics are not integrated.  

Some analyses note over-romanticization of inventors without addressing real-world moral costs of unchecked tech like Seraphim weapons.  

**Analysis:**  

Edison parallels historical Thomas Edison through tireless invention yet contrasts with pure-hearted loyalty to allies.  

In One Piece lore, he advances the "future island" motif by prioritizing knowledge escape over personal survival.  

Cross-domain links include creativity psychology (idea generation flow) and disaster recovery (backups like Punk Records).  

**Risks:**  

Over-reliance on singular "thinking" without balancing wisdom or action can cause tactical oversights.  

In real applications, unchecked innovation may ignore ethical implications or create dependency on fragmented teams.  

Distraction in high-pressure scenarios risks personal or group safety.

**Wise Perspectives:**  

Oda portrays fragmented genius as a metaphor for modern specialization, urging viewers to value intellect serving collective freedom over authoritarian control.  

As in systems thinking, isolated ideas gain power only through collaborative reconstruction, much like Edison's body rebuild.  

True innovation preserves knowledge for future generations rather than hoarding it.
