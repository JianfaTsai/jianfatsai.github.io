**Relevant Federal, State or Local Laws in Australia:**  
Wealth-building activities must comply with federal frameworks primarily, as state laws (such as Victoria’s consumer protections) defer to Commonwealth legislation in financial and IP matters. Maximum penalties apply for breaches, with penalty units valued at approximately AU$330 as of 2026.  

Corporations Act 2001 (Cth): Serious breaches, including misleading conduct or improper financial record-keeping, attract maximum criminal penalties of 15 years’ imprisonment and/or fines up to the greater of 4,500 penalty units (approximately AU$1.485 million), three times the benefit gained or loss avoided, or 10 percent of annual turnover for corporations (Australian Securities and Investments Commission, 2025).  

Income Tax Assessment Act 1997 (Cth) and Taxation Administration Act 1953 (Cth): Tax evasion or promoter penalties for schemes carry up to 10 years’ imprisonment and fines up to 2,000 penalty units (approximately AU$660,000) for individuals, or higher civil penalties of 75 percent of tax shortfall doubled for large entities (Australian Taxation Office, 2025).  

Copyright Act 1968 (Cth) and Patents Act 1990 (Cth): Criminal infringement of IP (such as counterfeiting trademarks or unauthorised use) incurs maximum penalties of 5 years’ imprisonment and fines up to AU$115,500 per offence for individuals (IP Australia, 2025).  

Foreign Acquisitions and Takeovers Act 1975 (Cth): Breaches involving unapproved investments yield civil penalties up to AU$33.3 million for corporations or 10 years’ imprisonment (Australian Taxation Office & Foreign Investment Review Board, 2025).  

Anti-Money Laundering and Counter-Terrorism Financing Act 2006 (Cth): Serious money laundering offences linked to undisclosed wealth sources carry a maximum penalty of life imprisonment or fines of up to AU$6.66 million for individuals where property exceeds AU$10 million (Australian Transaction Reports and Analysis Centre, 2025).  

Superannuation Industry (Supervision) Act 1993 (Cth): Non-compliant self-managed super fund activities trigger administrative penalties of 10–60 penalty units (approximately AU$3,300–AU$19,800) per breach or taxation at 45 percent on earnings (Australian Taxation Office, 2025).
