**Glossary:**

**Sexual penetration:** Introduction of a penis into the vagina, anus, or mouth, or any object or body part into the vagina or anus. 

**Consent:** Free and voluntary agreement to the act, ongoing and capable of being withdrawn at any time. 

**Mens rea:** The mental element required, such as knowledge of non-consent, recklessness, or absence of reasonable belief in consent. 

**Aggravating factors:** Circumstances that increase offence seriousness, such as use of violence, victim vulnerability, or multiple perpetrators. 

**Background Information:**

Criminal responsibility for rape resides with state and territory governments due to the Australian constitutional framework. 

Historical common law definitions were narrow and gender-specific, but modern reforms have made offences gender-neutral and consent-focused. 

Recent legislative changes across jurisdictions have strengthened affirmative consent standards requiring positive steps to ascertain consent. 

**Relevant Federal, State or Local Laws in Australia:**

**Victoria (applicable to user in Melbourne):** Crimes Act 1958 s38 defines rape as intentional sexual penetration where the complainant does not consent and the accused does not reasonably believe consent exists. 

The maximum penalty is 25 years’ imprisonment. 

**New South Wales:** Crimes Act 1900 s61I covers sexual intercourse without consent with maximum penalty of 14 years (aggravated offences up to 20 years or life). 

**Queensland:** Criminal Code s349 defines rape with maximum penalty of life imprisonment. 

**South Australia:** Criminal Law Consolidation Act 1935 s48 rape offence carries maximum life imprisonment. 

**Western Australia:** Criminal Code s325 sexual penetration without consent maximum 14 years (aggravated 20 years). 

**Tasmania Northern Territory and Australian Capital Territory:** Equivalent offences carry maxima ranging from 21 years to life depending on specifics. 

No overarching federal criminal code applies to rape as it is a state matter.
