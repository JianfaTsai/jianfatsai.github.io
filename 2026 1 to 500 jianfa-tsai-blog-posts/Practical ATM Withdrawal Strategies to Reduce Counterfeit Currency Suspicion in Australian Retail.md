## Federal, State, or Local Laws in Australia

Under the Crimes (Currency) Act 1981 (Cth), making, possessing, or using counterfeit money with intent to defraud constitutes an indictable offence carrying penalties of up to 14 years imprisonment for individuals (Australian Federal Police [AFP], n.d.; Crimes (Currency) Act 1981, s 6-9). No federal, state, or local statute prohibits withdrawing ninety dollars from an ATM or sharing general awareness advice about legitimate cash-handling practices, provided such actions do not involve knowingly distributing counterfeits (AFP, n.d.).
