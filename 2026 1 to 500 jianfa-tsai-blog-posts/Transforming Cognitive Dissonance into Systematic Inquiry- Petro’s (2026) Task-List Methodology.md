### Action Steps
1. During your next reading session, prepare a dedicated notebook or digital document labeled “Confusion Task List.”
2. As you encounter each unclear concept, pause only long enough to jot a brief note (keyword or phrase) without stopping your overall reading flow.
3. Continue reading until your scheduled session ends, treating the list as a promise to your future self rather than an immediate emergency.
4. Immediately after the session, transfer the list into a calendar or task-management app and block a specific 30–60-minute review window within 48 hours.
5. In the review block, research each item using peer-reviewed sources first, then apply the Feynman technique by explaining each concept aloud in your own words.
6. Synthesize findings by connecting new insights back to the original text through marginal notes or a summary paragraph.
7. Reflect metacognitively: note which types of confusion arose most frequently and adjust future reading strategies accordingly.
8. Repeat weekly, tracking progress in a learning journal to observe improvements in reading stamina and comprehension depth.
