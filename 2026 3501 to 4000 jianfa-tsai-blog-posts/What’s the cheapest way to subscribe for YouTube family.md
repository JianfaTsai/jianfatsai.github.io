What’s the cheapest way to subscribe for YouTube family   
  
The cheapest way to get YouTube Premium Family often depends on **where** you sign up and **how** you pay. As of April 2026, YouTube has recently increased prices in several regions, making the hunt for a deal even more relevant.  
Here is the breakdown of the most cost-effective strategies:  
### 1. Avoid the "Apple Tax" (Most Important)  
If you subscribe through the YouTube app on an **iPhone or iPad**, Apple takes a 30% cut, and Google passes that cost to you.  
 * **The Fix:** Sign up via a **web browser** (on a computer or mobile browser) at youtube.com/premium.  
 * **Savings:** In the US, this usually saves you about **$7–$9 per month** compared to the App Store price.  
### 2. Regional Pricing (The "VPN Method")  
YouTube prices its service based on local purchasing power. Some users use a VPN to "relocate" their account to a country with lower rates.  
 * **Cheapest Countries (Approx. Monthly Family Plan):**  
   * **Argentina:** ~$1.45 USD  
   * **India:** ~$2.27 USD  
   * **Turkey:** ~$3.40 USD  
   * **Algeria/Ukraine:** ~$3.50 – $6.30 USD  
 * **The Catch:** YouTube has become very strict about this. You often need a **credit card issued in that specific country** and a matching billing address. If caught, your Premium subscription may be cancelled.  
### 3. Maximize the "Family" in Family Plan  
The Family Plan allows you to add up to **5 additional members** (6 total) who live in your "household."  
 * **The Math:** At the new US rate of **$26.99/month**, splitting this with five friends or family members brings the cost down to roughly **$4.50 per person**.  
 * **Note:** Google occasionally checks that family members live at the same address via IP pings, though this is enforced less strictly than Netflix's recent crackdowns.  
### 4. Check for Bundle Deals  
Sometimes the cheapest way isn't through YouTube directly:  
 * **Mobile Carriers:** Some T-Mobile, Verizon, or EE (UK) plans include YouTube Premium or offer it as a discounted "Add-on" for $10/month.  
 * **Google One:** Check if your Google One storage plan offers any "member benefits" or trials for YouTube Premium.
