**Detailed analysis and summary**    
The City of Melbourne Libraries (CoML) is the municipal library service provided by the City of Melbourne council offering borrowing of various materials to support community access and education. Languages Other Than English (LOTE) refers to materials available in non-English languages such as Chinese, Hindi, Japanese and Korean to serve diverse populations. The policy (last revised August 2024, next review June 2025) establishes a clear framework for lending physical items (books, DVDs, magazines, CDs, language kits, console games, maker kits, accessible equipment) and digital resources (eBooks, eAudiobooks, streaming via platforms like BorrowBox, Libby, Kanopy, Beama Films, Freegal) across all CoML service points including the City, East Melbourne, North Melbourne, Southbank, Kathleen Syme libraries and Library at The Dock.    
  
Core rules promote efficiency and fairness:    
- **Membership and eligibility** — All Victorian residents qualify for free full membership; short-term options exist for interstate/overseas visitors. Unverified members (proof of address pending) are limited to 2 physical items plus eResources. Institutional/organisational members may borrow up to 50 items per card. Parents/guardians are fully responsible for items borrowed by anyone under 16. Lost cards must be reported immediately or liability stays with the member.    
- **Loan periods, quotas and limits** (maximum 50 items total per full member) — Most physical formats (books, magazines, language kits, ESL materials, CDs, DVDs) allow 21 days with up to 4 renewals. Hot Picks books are restricted to 7 days with 0 renewals. Console games (3 items), maker kits (2 items), accessible equipment (3 items) and ESL testing kits (10 items) have tighter quotas. Digital limits vary: Cloud eBooks/eAudiobooks (10 items, varies by renewal), BorrowBox (10 eBooks/8 eAudiobooks), Libby (10 eBooks/eAudio combined + unlimited eMagazines if unreserved), Kanopy (5 films per month for 3 days), Beama Films and Freegal (unlimited streaming with download caps). Reference, local history, newspapers and current-issue magazines are not for loan. Age restrictions apply: R-rated material only for 18+, MA (Mature Audiences) for 15+.    
- **Reservations and renewals** — Maximum 20 reservations at once; items held for 7 days on the shelf. Renewals (up to 4) are unavailable for Hot Picks, reserved items, or during system downtime.    
- **Interlibrary Loan (ILL)** — Interlibrary Loan (ILL) is the process of requesting materials from other libraries not in the local collection. Limited to 2 requests at a time; free from Victorian public libraries but $32 fee for university/special libraries. No renewals; restrictions apply for members with charges over $10, overdue items or unverified status.    
- **Notices, overdues and debt recovery** — Courtesy reminders (email/SMS/mail) sent 3/5/7 days before due date, then at 1 day, 7 days, 21 days and 42 days overdue. Matters over $10 may go to debt recovery.    
- **Lost, damaged and partial returns** — Charges use depreciated value (the reduced worth of an item after accounting for age, wear, and usage) up to full replacement cost; no replacement copies accepted and no refunds if the item is later found (member keeps it). Water-damaged print items are automatically charged due to mould risk. Partial check-ins trigger follow-up; all components must be returned or full item is charged. Claims Returned/Claims Never Borrowed require either payment or a witnessed Statutory Declaration (a written statement of facts declared to be true by the signer in the presence of an authorised witness, carrying legal weight) within 28 days.    
- **Member charges and Attachment 1** — Charges are guided by legislation, State Government funding rules, Council Plan principles, equity for diverse communities, industry consistency and the distinction between “basic” free public library services (free entrance, access to collections in any format, lending, reference help, training, programs) versus “value-added” services (e.g. remote paid databases, ILL from non-public networks, printing, reservations in some cases, office software use) that may incur fees.    
- **Responsibilities and edge cases** — Members must comply or risk suspension/bar; authorised staff handle exceptions. Policy applies to online collections and covers downtime, barcode-less items, and equity considerations for disability or special needs.  
  
Nuances and implications include strong emphasis on self-service (app/website for reservations/renewals), proactive notifications to reduce overdues, and protection of the collection (no water damage tolerance, strict lost-item policy). Edge cases covered: unverified members, minors, institutional users, age-rated material, system downtime, and failed ILL collections. Overall the policy balances generous access (50-item cap, multiple formats/languages) with accountability to ensure materials circulate efficiently for the whole community.
