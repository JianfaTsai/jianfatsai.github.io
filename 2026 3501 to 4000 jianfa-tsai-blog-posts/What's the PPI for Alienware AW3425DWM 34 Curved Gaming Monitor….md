What's the PPI for "Alienware AW3425DWM 34" Curved Gaming Monitor, QHD 3440 x 1440 up to 180 Hz, 2ms Response Time, AMD FreeSync, VESA DisplayHDR 400, Height Adjustable" and how does this compare to the Dell UltraSharp 49 Curved USB-C Hub Monitor - U4924DW. What are the pros and cons for me using two of the above Alienware monitors (I only need to buy one more, since I already have one existing" versus the DELL 49 inch monitor above?  
  
**Alienware AW3425DWM PPI: 110.**    
Dell U4924DW PPI: 109.    
  
They are **virtually identical** in pixel density/sharpness.  
  
### Pros of two Alienware AW3425DWM (you buy one more) vs one Dell U4924DW  
- **Much better for gaming**: 180 Hz + 2 ms response vs Dell’s 60 Hz.    
- **More total screen space**: 6880×1440 pixels across two monitors vs Dell’s 5120×1440.    
- **Flexible setup**: easy to game on one + work on the other; matched monitors (same colour/curve).    
- **More immersive curve** (1500R vs Dell’s gentler 3800R).    
  
### Cons of two Alienware vs one Dell  
- **Bezel in the middle** breaks seamless workflow.    
- **Two stands, cables, and power bricks** — more desk clutter.    
- **Gaming-focused panel** (VA): slightly worse text clarity and viewing angles than Dell’s IPS Black.    
- **No built-in USB-C hub** (Dell has 90 W PD + KVM for easy laptop docking).    
  
**Quick verdict (simple):**    
Choose **two Alienware** if you game a lot or want max width/flexibility.    
Choose the **Dell** if you want one clean, ultra-wide desktop for productivity/multitasking.
