**Dual QHD (DQHD)** is Dual Quad High Definition, referring to a 5120x1440 resolution that effectively provides the horizontal space of two QHD monitors in one panel.    
**PPI (Pixels Per Inch)** measures pixel density; higher values deliver sharper text and images at typical viewing distances.    
**KVM Switch** is a Keyboard Video Mouse switch, allowing one set of peripherals to control multiple computers seamlessly.  
  
Samsung's 49-inch lineup (primarily the Odyssey G9 series and ViewFinity S9/S95UC variants) stands out for ultrawide productivity. For crisp text reading, prioritize **VA panel** models over OLED because VA offers superior native contrast and typically sharper font rendering without the subpixel layout issues common in QD-OLED panels that can cause slight color fringing or perceived blur on fine text edges. Dual QHD resolution at 49 inches yields approximately 110 PPI, which is sufficient for clear text when using proper OS scaling (avoid 100% to prevent tiny fonts; test 125-150%).
