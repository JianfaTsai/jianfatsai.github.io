### Executive Summary (ELI5)

The WordPress Community Code of Conduct is a set of "golden rules" designed to make sure everyone feels safe and welcome while working together, promising that being mean or harassing others will lead to specific consequences like warnings or being banned.

### Core Principles and Expectations

The WordPress community commits to a harassment-free experience for all participants regardless of personal characteristics such as age, disability, ethnicity, or identity. The guidelines emphasize fostering an inclusive and healthy environment.

**Positive Behaviors:**Positive Behaviors

- **Empathy:**Empathy: Demonstrating kindness toward others.
    
- **Respect:**Respect: Honoring differing viewpoints and experiences.
    
- **Accountability:**Accountability: Accepting responsibility for mistakes and learning from them.
    
- **Community Focus:**Community Focus: Prioritizing the collective good over individual interests.
    

**Unacceptable Behaviors:**Unacceptable Behaviors

- Using sexualized language or making unwanted sexual advances.
    
- Engaging in personal or political attacks, baiting, or insulting comments.
    
- Doxing (publishing private information without permission).
    
- Retaliating against individuals who report concerns in good faith.
    

### Scope and Enforcement Responsibilities

The Code of Conduct applies to all community spaces, both virtual and in-person, as well as instances where an individual officially represents the WordPress community. Community leaders, including team representatives and organizers, are responsible for enforcing these standards and have the authority to remove or reject contributions that do not align with the codeCommunity leaders, including team representatives and organizers, are responsible for enforcing these standards and have the authority to remove or reject contributions that do not align with the code.

### Enforcement Ladder

Violations are handled through a tiered system of consequences based on the impact of the behavior:

|Level|Community Impact|Consequence|
|---|---|---|
|**1. Correction**|Inappropriate or unprofessional language.|Private written warning and potential request for public apology.|
|**2. Warning**|Single incident or series of actions.|Warning with a specified period of "no interaction" with involved parties.|
|**3. Temporary Ban**|Serious or sustained inappropriate behavior.|Temporary ban from all community communication and interaction.|
|**4. Permanent Ban**|Pattern of violation, harassment, or aggression toward groups.|Permanent removal from all public interaction within the community.|

### Counter-Argument

While a strict Code of Conduct ensures safety, critics sometimes argue that subjective enforcement (e.g., what constitutes a "political attack" or "unprofessional behavior") can lead to "tone policing." This may inadvertently discourage passionate debate or marginalize contributors from cultures with different communication norms, potentially centralizing power within the hands of the "Community Leaders" who interpret the rules.
