**Glossary:**  
Evidence Management: Creation, alteration, or elimination of materials to support or obscure narratives.  
Rendition: Covert transfer of individuals to jurisdictions with fewer legal protections.  
Black Site: Undisclosed facility for detention and interrogation outside standard oversight.  
Perverting the Course of Justice: Intentional act that obstructs or defeats judicial processes.  
Chain of Custody: Documented trail preserving evidence integrity (contrasted here with deliberate breaks).

**Relevant Federal, State or Local Laws in Australia:**  
In Victoria the common-law offence of perverting the course of justice (recognised under s 320 Crimes Act 1958) carries a maximum penalty of 25 years imprisonment for acts such as destroying or fabricating evidence to obstruct justice.  
Section 254 of the Crimes Act 1958 specifically prohibits destruction or concealment of any document or thing likely to be required as evidence in a legal proceeding (maximum 5 years imprisonment).  
Federally the Criminal Code Act 1995 contains espionage and foreign interference provisions that criminalise unauthorised dealing with national security information but do not authorise evidence tampering outside strict statutory powers.
