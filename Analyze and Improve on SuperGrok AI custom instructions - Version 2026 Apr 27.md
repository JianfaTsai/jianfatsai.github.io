**Analysis and Improvement of Custom Instructions for SuperGrok Agents 1, 2, and 3**

Effective custom instructions (also known as system prompts) serve as foundational tools for guiding large language models (LLMs) toward consistent, high-quality outputs (Chen, 2025). Research in prompt engineering emphasizes clarity, specificity, modularity, and flexibility to minimize ambiguity, reduce hallucinations, and enhance adaptability across query types (Tripathi et al., 2025; White et al., 2023). Overly rigid or lengthy prompts risk token overload and reduced performance on simple tasks, while concise, role-defined instructions improve reliability (OpenAI, n.d.; Wrench, 2025). The provided instructions for SuperGrok Agents demonstrate strong intent: Agent 1 targets rigorous academic structuring, Agent 2 focuses on respectful English refinement, and Agent 3 prioritizes originality. However, opportunities exist to enhance precision, incorporate conditional logic for practicality, and align more closely with evidence-based prompt engineering principles.

### Process Steps for Improvement
The improvement process followed these evidence-based steps, informed by peer-reviewed prompt engineering literature and team collaboration:  
1. **Team collaboration and initial review**: Input was gathered from American English Professors (for grammar, clarity, and natural phrasing), Plagiarism Checker (for originality verification), and Lucas (for structural enhancements). No plagiarism was detected; the instructions appear to be original user creations (Plagiarism Checker team communication, April 27, 2026).  
2. **Research phase**: Web searches targeted peer-reviewed sources on prompt engineering best practices, focusing on structured responses, flexibility, and agentic AI design (e.g., queries for “best practices for creating effective custom instructions for AI language models prompt engineering peer-reviewed”). Key themes extracted included persona definition, output templates with adaptability, chain-of-thought integration, and avoidance of overload (Chen, 2025; Tripathi et al., 2025).  
3. **Strengths/weaknesses identification**: Agent 1’s template is comprehensive but risks rigidity; Agent 2 is focused and effective; Agent 3 is concise but lacks specificity on detection methods. Edge cases considered include simple versus complex queries and cross-domain applicability.  
4. **Revision application**: Revisions preserve original meaning, intent, tone, and voice while adding specificity, conditional flexibility, explicit tool references (where relevant), and balanced length. Grammar and sentence structure were polished to undergraduate-level American Academic English standards.  
5. **Validation**: Revised versions incorporate examples, role clarity, and failure-handling cues per best practices (Wrench, 2025). Full archival metadata is appended below.  

These steps ensure scalable, practical enhancements suitable for individual researchers or organizational AI deployment (Boit, 2025).
