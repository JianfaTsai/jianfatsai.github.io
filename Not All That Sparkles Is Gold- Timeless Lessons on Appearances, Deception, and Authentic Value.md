Early versions of this wisdom appeared in 12th-century writings, but Shakespeare gave it lasting fame in his play *The Merchant of Venice*. In today's world, the idea applies to everything from social media posts to product ads. This article analyzes the proverb's meaning, its relevance in Australia, and ways to apply it thoughtfully (Johnson, 2013).  

**Federal, State, or Local Laws in Australia**  
Australian laws protect consumers from misleading appearances under the Australian Consumer Law, which is part of the Competition and Consumer Act 2010 (Cth). Section 18 bans conduct that is deceptive or misleading, such as false advertising that makes products seem more valuable than they are (Australian Competition and Consumer Commission, n.d.). State laws, like those in Victoria, add extra rules for fair trading. These rules stop businesses from using shiny packaging or claims that hide the truth about quality or safety.
