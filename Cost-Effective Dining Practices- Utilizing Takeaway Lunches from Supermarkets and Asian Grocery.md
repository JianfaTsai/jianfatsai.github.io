### Federal, State, or Local Laws in Australia

In Victoria, the Food Act 1984 governs food businesses rather than individual consumers, focusing on preparation, storage, and sale standards enforced by local councils (Victorian Department of Health, 2024). Shopping centers operate as private property, where management may set conditions of entry; however, no statewide or local statute in Victoria explicitly bans outside food in food court seating areas, as these spaces function as common areas (forum analyses of property contracts confirm this permissive stance).
